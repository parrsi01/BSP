# Team Revenue Split & Business Structure

How BSP (4 producers) splits income fairly, tracks contributions, manages money, and protects the business — without needing a lawyer to understand it.

---

## Overview

The goal is a system that is:
- **Fair:** Income reflects actual contribution
- **Transparent:** Everyone sees the numbers
- **Simple:** Low admin overhead
- **Written:** Verbal agreements cause team breakdowns — document everything

---

## Section 1: Split Models

| Model | How It Works | Best For | Weakness |
|-------|-------------|---------|---------|
| Equal split (25/25/25/25) | All income split equally regardless of contribution | Teams with truly equal workload and output | Demotivating if one member contributes significantly more |
| Contribution-based | Track who made what; split per beat or per asset | Teams where individual output varies significantly | High admin burden; can create resentment if tracked poorly |
| Role-based | Each defined role gets a fixed % of total team revenue | Teams with clear specialization | Doesn't account for output volume within a role |
| Hybrid (recommended) | Base percentage (equal) + variable percentage (by contribution) | Most real teams | Requires agreed tracking metrics |

### Recommended Model for BSP: Hybrid

**Structure:**
- 40% of total revenue split equally (25/25/25/25 of the 40%) — baseline for team membership
- 60% of total revenue split by measured contribution

**Why this works:** The equal base ensures everyone benefits from team brand-building, shared marketing, and infrastructure maintenance (tasks that don't produce direct revenue). The variable portion ensures the producer who made 8 beats this month earns more than the one who made 2.

## Section 2: Role-Based Split Suggestion

If each team member has a clearly defined primary role, this alternative works well:

| Role | Revenue Share | Responsibilities |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Primary Beat Maker | 30% | Produces majority of catalog beats, sets sound direction |
| Secondary Beat Maker | 25% | Produces supporting catalog beats, assists primary |
| Marketing Lead | 25% | Manages all social media, paid ads, beat store optimization, outreach |
| Visual / Graphic Designer | 20% | Cover art, video content, brand identity, waveform videos |

**Total: 100%**

**Adjust quarterly** based on whether each member is actually fulfilling their role. If the Marketing Lead is not posting content, their share should be reviewed at the next quarterly meeting.

**Key principle:** A role-based split only works if roles are clearly defined and each person is accountable. Document deliverables per role explicitly.

## Section 3: Tracking Contributions

Objective tracking prevents disputes. Use the simplest method the team will actually maintain.

### Beats Produced — Tracking Method

| Method | Tool | Effort |
|--------|------|--------|
| File naming convention (see WORKFLOW.md) | Built into file names | Zero extra effort |
| Shared Google Sheet | Google Sheets | Low |
| Notion production log | Notion | Low–Medium |
| Git commit log (if using repo for beat project files) | Git | Low if already in workflow |

**Minimum log fields per beat:**
- Beat name
- Producer(s) who made it (if collab, note contribution %)
- Date completed
- Status: In Progress / Ready for Mix / Released / Sold

### Marketing Assets — Tracking Method

Each piece of content created should be logged in a shared document:

| Asset | Creator | Platform | Date | Engagement (30-day) |
|-------|---------|---------|------|---------------------|
| Reel — City Lights beat | Member B | Instagram | 2025-03-01 | 12K views |
| Cover art — Galaxy EP | Member D | All platforms | 2025-03-05 | — |
| TikTok — Studio session | Member C | TikTok | 2025-03-07 | 8K views |

### Monthly Contribution Review

**Agenda for monthly team meeting (15 minutes max):**
1. Review beats made in the past month — log against each member
2. Review marketing assets created — log against each member
3. Total revenue for the month — from all streams
4. Calculate each member's share based on the agreed model
5. Transfer payments from team account to individual accounts

## Section 4: Bank Account Setup

**Option 1: Joint Business Bank Account**

Open a business checking account in the LLC's name (once you form one). All revenue flows in, all expenses paid from it, distributions to members monthly.

Recommended banks for small creative businesses:
- Relay (relay.fi) — free business banking, multiple sub-accounts for budgeting, great for small teams
- Mercury (mercury.com) — free, tech-friendly, supports multi-user access
- Local credit union — lower fees, personal service

**Option 2: PayPal Business (Free)**

Free to set up, accepts PayPal transfers, BeatStars payouts, and most payment methods. Limitations: less professional for clients, currency conversion fees for international income, no multi-user account management.

**Option 3: Wise Business (formerly TransferWise)**

Useful if team members are in different countries or receiving international payments. Holds multiple currencies, low conversion fees.

### Revenue Flow Structure

```
All Platform Payouts (BeatStars, DistroKid, SoundCloud, Fiverr)
                    ↓
           BSP Team Business Account
                    ↓
        Monthly Distribution to Members
           (per agreed split model)
```

**Keep in the business account at all times:**
- Emergency fund: 1 month of platform subscription costs
- Tax reserve: 25–30% of revenue set aside for self-employment taxes

## Section 5: Simple Partnership Agreement Outline

This does not need to be a formal legal document to start — a shared Google Doc signed (digitally or by scanning signatures) by all 4 members is legally enforceable as a written agreement in most jurisdictions.

### What to Cover

**1. Business Identity**
- Legal name of the partnership or LLC: BSP / [full legal name]
- State of formation (if LLC)
- Date of agreement
- Names and addresses of all members

**2. Income Split**
- The chosen model (hybrid / role-based / equal)
- Exact percentages per member
- Review frequency (recommend: quarterly)
- Process to change percentages (unanimous agreement required)

**3. Decision Making**
| Decision Type | Process Required |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Day-to-day operations | Any member can act |
| Spending over $100 | Majority vote (3 of 4) |
| Signing any contract | Majority vote |
| Exclusive beat sale over $500 | Unanimous agreement |
| Adding/removing a member | Unanimous agreement |
| Dissolving the partnership | Unanimous agreement |

**4. What Happens If a Member Leaves**
- Buyout formula: departing member receives their % of the previous 12 months' net revenue as a buyout
- IP: beats they created remain BSP property; they retain their solo producer credits
- Transition period: 30-day notice required
- Non-disparagement clause: no public negative statements about the team or remaining members

**5. New Members**
- Any new member requires unanimous agreement
- New member's percentage comes out of the equal base pool (the 40%)
- Updated agreement required and signed by all members including new member

**6. Dispute Resolution**
1. Direct conversation between parties (7 days)
2. Mediation with a neutral third party (30 days)
3. Binding arbitration (per state rules)
4. Small claims court (for disputes under state threshold)

## Section 6: Beat Ownership Rules

Clear ownership rules prevent the most common team breakdowns.

### Solo-Made Beats

| Scenario | Who Owns the Beat | Team Revenue Share |
|----------|------------------|--------------------|
| Beat made alone by Member A | Member A owns master + composition | Team gets % from marketing and distribution support |
| Beat made alone, released under BSP | BSP owns the master; Member A is credited composer | Revenue split per team agreement |
| Beat sold exclusively | BSP retains publishing; master transfers to buyer | Sale proceeds split per team agreement |

**Recommended:** All beats made by any member and intended for commercial release under the BSP brand are BSP property (master rights jointly owned). Individual member retains their composition credit and the associated PRO royalties.

### Collaboration Beats

| Scenario | Split |
|----------|-------|
| Two members collab — equal contribution | 50/50 composition, 50/50 master |
| Two members collab — one did 70% of the work | Split per agreed contribution % at time of creation |
| Guest producer outside the team | Agreed in writing before starting — document in a collab split sheet |

### Template: Quick Beat Split Sheet

```
BEAT COLLABORATION SPLIT SHEET

Beat Title: _______________
Date Made: _______________

Contributor 1: ____________ | Master: ___% | Publishing: ___%
Contributor 2: ____________ | Master: ___% | Publishing: ___%
(Add rows as needed)

Total must equal 100% for each.

All parties agree this split is final for this beat.

Signatures: _____________ / _____________ Date: _______
```

### Masters Retention Policy

**BSP retains master rights on all releases unless:**
- An exclusive sale contract explicitly transfers master rights to the buyer
- A major label deal or distribution deal includes a master licensing provision (get legal advice before signing any such deal)
- A work-for-hire arrangement is agreed upon in writing at a premium rate

**Streaming masters:** All distributed beat tapes and singles retain BSP as master rights holder. Artists who lease beats do not acquire master rights — this is stated in every lease agreement.

### Publishing Allocation

Each member's publishing share flows to their individual PRO registration. In a collaborative beat:
- Each member registers their composition share with their own PRO account
- Both use the same PRO (ASCAP or BMI — team should standardize on one)
- PRO registration lists all contributors and their percentages

**Important:** PRO royalties are paid to individuals, not to a business entity (unless the business is registered as a publishing entity). Ensure each member has both a writer account and a publisher affiliate account with the same PRO.

## Section 7: Quarterly Business Review

Run this every 3 months (15–30 minute meeting):

| Agenda Item | Data Needed |
|-------------|-------------|
| Revenue summary | Total income by stream source |
| Expense summary | Subscriptions, ads, equipment |
| Net profit | Revenue minus expenses |
| Contribution audit | Beats made, marketing assets created, by member |
| Split recalculation | If using hybrid model, recalculate based on contribution data |
| Goal review | Did we hit last quarter's targets? |
| Goal setting | 3 measurable targets for next quarter |

**Suggested targets format:**
- Revenue: "Reach $X total revenue this quarter"
- Catalog: "Upload X new beats to BeatStars"
- Audience: "Grow SoundCloud followers to X"
- Skills: "Each member completes one production course this quarter"

## Appendix: Revenue Tracking Spreadsheet Structure

Minimum columns for a shared team revenue tracker (Google Sheets):

| Column | Data |
|--------|------|
| Date | Date of payment received |
| Source | BeatStars / DistroKid / Fiverr / Direct / etc. |
| Type | Lease / Exclusive / Streaming / Coaching / etc. |
| Gross Amount | Payment before fees |
| Platform Fee | BeatStars commission, PayPal fee, etc. |
| Net Amount | Gross minus fees |
| Beat/Project | Which beat or project generated this |
| Producer | Which member made the beat (for contribution tracking) |
| Split to M1 | Member 1's share |
| Split to M2 | Member 2's share |
| Split to M3 | Member 3's share |
| Split to M4 | Member 4's share |
| Notes | Any relevant context |

Run this as a shared Google Sheet with view access for all members and edit access for the designated Finance Lead.
