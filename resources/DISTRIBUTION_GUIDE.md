# Music Distribution — Getting Beats on Streaming Platforms

A complete guide for producer teams on distributing instrumentals, beat tapes, and compilations to streaming platforms and maximizing the revenue and discovery that comes with it.

---

## 1. Why Distribute Beats

**Passive royalties:** Every stream generates a small royalty. At scale, this is meaningful supplemental income with zero ongoing work after upload.

**Credibility:** A Spotify profile with a beat tape signals professionalism to artists browsing for producers. "Prod. by BSP" in a track becomes searchable on streaming platforms.

**Artist discovery:** Artists search Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube Music for beats in their genre. Distribution expands your reach beyond SoundCloud and BeatStars.

**PRO performance royalties:** Streaming platforms report plays to PROs. Once registered, your tracks generate performance royalties from Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and others — on top of the per-stream master royalty paid by the platform.

**Sync pipeline:** Having your beats on a legitimate streaming profile strengthens your case when pitching to sync libraries and supervisors. It demonstrates your catalog exists and is professionally released.

## 3. What to Release

### Beat Tapes as Albums
A collection of 8–15 beats with a cohesive theme, artwork, and intentional track listing. Released as an LP or EP.

**Structure:** Intro (shorter, cinematic) → 3–5 main beats → brief interlude → 3–5 more beats → outro. Total runtime 20–45 minutes.

**Naming convention:** "BSP — [Project Title] (Instrumentals)" or just the project title if it's a known EP.

### Individual Instrumentals as Singles
High-priority beats that stand alone — a trending sound, a strong hook, a specific genre bait (e.g., UK drill, Amapiano, Afrohouse). Release as singles to pitch to editorial playlists.

**Advantage:** Each single gets its own Spotify for Artists pitch window. Albums do not get pitched beat-by-beat.

### Riddim Compilations as EPs
Your riddim beat with 4–8 vocal artists — released as a 5–10 track EP. Credits producer prominently. See INCOME_STREAMS.md for the full setup.

**Distribution note:** For compilations with multiple artists, use DistroKid's "Albums" upload with "Various Artists" as the artist name, or under the riddim name as a project entity.

## 4. Metadata for Distribution — Do Not Guess

Bad metadata = lost royalties. Distributors pass metadata directly to DSPs, which use it to pay out correctly and serve your music to the right audience.

### ISRC Codes

**What they are:** International Standard Recording Code — a unique identifier for each sound recording. Format: `CC-XXX-YY-NNNNN` (country + registrant + year + designation).

**How to get them:** DistroKid, TuneCore, and CD Baby all assign ISRC codes automatically per track at no extra charge. If you are self-releasing, you can apply directly via the US ISRC Agency (RIAA) for $95/year for a registrant code.

**Why they matter:** Platforms report streams back to rights holders via ISRC. Without one, your streams may not be attributed. Your PRO also uses ISRC to match plays to registered works.

### UPC Codes

**What they are:** Universal Product Code — identifies the release (album, EP, or single) rather than individual tracks. Used by retailers and streaming platforms.

**How to get them:** Automatically assigned by your distributor per release. No action required.

### Genre Classification

Choose carefully. DSP algorithms use genre to surface your music in editorial playlists and recommendations.

- Be specific: "Afro House" beats instead of just "Electronic"
- Use subgenre fields where available: "Instrumental Hip-Hop", "Trap Soul", "UK Drill Instrumental"
- Primary genre and secondary genre slots — use both

### Contributor Credits

Fill in every available field:
- Primary artist: BSP (or your producer name)
- Composer: All contributing producers by legal name
- Lyricist: N/A for instrumentals (leave blank or "Instrumental")
- Producer: BSP / individual producer name
- ISNI or IPN number: Optional, but useful for royalty matching

**Important for team releases:** All four members who contributed to a beat should be listed as composers. Royalty splits are determined by your split sheet, not by who has billing in the metadata — but proper metadata ensures all names are associated with the recording in databases.

## 5. Releasing Instrumentals on Spotify

### Playlist Pitch Process

Spotify for Artists allows you to pitch one upcoming unreleased track to the editorial team per release cycle. This must be done **at least 7 days before release date** — ideally 3–4 weeks out.

**How to pitch:**
1. Go to artists.spotify.com → Music → Upcoming
2. Select the unreleased track (it must be distributed and scheduled)
3. Complete the pitch form: mood, style, instrumentation, story
4. Submit — you will not receive individual feedback, but tracks can land on editorial playlists

### Targeting Editorial Playlists for Instrumentals

| Playlist Name | Genre | Monthly Listeners |
|--------------|-------|-------------------|
| Beats | Hip-Hop instrumentals | High |
| Instrumental Hip-Hop | Boom bap, lo-fi adjacent | Medium |
| Electronic Focus | Electronic production | High |
| Sono Indie | Indie electronic | Medium |
| Nightfall | Atmospheric, cinematic | High |
| Atmospheric Calm | Ambient, background | Medium |

**Pitch angle for instrumentals:** Emphasize mood, tempo, and use case ("ideal for late-night creative sessions", "high-energy club-ready production"). Most editorial playlists are more receptive to instrumentals than many producers expect.

### Independent Playlist Pitching

Beyond editorial, pitch to curators directly:

- SubmitHub: submit tracks to independent playlist curators (paid and free credits)
- Groover: similar to SubmitHub, strong in electronic/hip-hop
- Indiemono: free submissions for lo-fi and chill adjacent
- Direct Instagram DM to playlist curators in your genre

## 6. Collecting Royalties

### Streaming Royalty Rates — Per-Stream Estimates (2024–2025)

| Platform | Per Stream (approximate) | Notes |
|----------|-------------------------|-------|
| Spotify | $0.003–$0.005 | Varies by listener's country and plan |
| Apple Music | $0.007–$0.010 | Higher per-stream rate |
| Amazon Music | $0.004–$0.006 | Varies by tier |
| Tidal | $0.010–$0.013 | Highest among major platforms |
| YouTube Music | $0.001–$0.003 | Lowest among major platforms |
| Deezer | $0.003–$0.006 | Fan-centric model being phased in |
| Pandora | $0.001–$0.003 | Low rate, limited international reach |

These are **master royalties** paid to the rights holder of the recording — distributed to you via your distributor.

**Publishing royalties (separate):** Spotify, Apple Music, and others also pay mechanical royalties on compositions. These are collected via your publishing admin (Songtrust, DistroKid Publishing, CD Baby Pro). Without registering with a publishing admin, you leave mechanical royalties uncollected.

### Payment Timeline

| Distributor | When They Pay Out | Minimum Payout |
|-------------|------------------|----------------|
| DistroKid | After platforms report (monthly, 30–90 day lag) | $0 (direct to bank) |
| TuneCore | Monthly | $0 |
| CD Baby | Monthly | $0 |
| Amuse | Monthly | $0 |

Streaming royalties typically have a 2–4 month lag between when a stream occurs and when you receive payment. Budget accordingly — income from a January release may not arrive until March or April.

### SoundExchange

SoundExchange collects **digital performance royalties** on behalf of featured artists and master rights holders for non-interactive digital broadcasts (Pandora, SiriusXM, iHeartRadio, internet radio).

**Important:** SoundExchange royalties are separate from interactive streaming royalties (Spotify, Apple Music). Your distributor does NOT collect SoundExchange royalties.

**Action item:** Register at soundexchange.com as a rights holder. Free to register. They hold royalties for unregistered rights holders for up to 3 years before distributing to a general pool.

## 7. YouTube Content ID

### What It Is

Content ID is YouTube's automated system that scans uploaded videos against a database of registered audio. When a match is found, the rights holder can: claim ad revenue, block the video, or track views.

### How It Works for Producers

1. You (or your distributor) register your beats with Content ID
2. When any YouTube user uploads a video using your beat (with or without permission), YouTube detects it
3. You can automatically claim ad revenue from those videos

### Setting Up Content ID via DistroKid

DistroKid offers "YouTube Money + Content ID" as an add-on ($4.95/year per release). This registers each release with Content ID through DistroKid's partnership.

**Alternatively:** Deliver directly to YouTube via DistroKid's YouTube Music distribution (separate from Content ID) or use AdRev/Identifyy for standalone Content ID registration.

### Critical Warning: Content ID and Beat Leasing Conflict

If an artist purchases a lease for your beat and uploads their song to YouTube, Content ID will claim their video — redirecting ad revenue to you instead of them.

**This is a serious customer service problem.** Options to manage:

| Approach | Benefit | Drawback |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Don't enroll leased beats in Content ID | Artists have no claim conflict | You miss revenue from unlicensed uses |
| Whitelist known buyers manually | Buyers are protected | Requires manual management per buyer |
| Use DistroKid's Whitelisting (beta) | Semi-automated | Not all distributors offer this cleanly |
| Only enroll exclusive sales | Minimal conflict (one buyer) | Leaves most Content ID revenue uncaptured |

**BSP Recommendation:** Only enroll sample-free beats that have not been leased in Content ID. For leased beats, rely on DistroKid streaming revenue instead. State your Content ID policy clearly in your license terms.

## 8. Free Distribution Options

### Amuse (Free Tier)

Amuse offers free distribution to major platforms (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, etc.) with 100% royalty retention. Limitations: slower processing (2–4 weeks), limited to 40+ platforms, no priority support.

**Best for:** Releasing a few beats to test the market before committing to a paid plan.

### SoundCloud Go+ / SoundCloud Distribution

SoundCloud Pro Unlimited ($16/month) includes distribution to Spotify and Apple Music via their partnership with Repost Network. Royalty share is 100% of SoundCloud streams + 80% of distributed platform streams (20% to SoundCloud).

**Best for:** Teams already committed to SoundCloud as their primary platform who want a single subscription to manage both.

### Bandcamp

Not a traditional streaming platform (not on Spotify/Apple), but Bandcamp is free to use, takes 15% of sales (dropping to 10% after $5,000 cumulative sales), and pays out quickly. Ideal for beat tape direct sales and supporting fans who want to pay more than streaming provides.

**Bandcamp Fridays:** Bandcamp waives their fee on designated Fridays — time your releases accordingly for maximum artist/producer payout.

## Distribution Release Checklist

Before clicking submit on any distribution platform:

```
[ ] Audio file: 44.1kHz, 16-bit or 24-bit WAV, no clipping, -14 LUFS integrated
[ ] Artwork: 3000x3000px minimum, JPG or PNG, no blurriness, no text cut off
[ ] Title: Final, exactly as it should appear on all platforms
[ ] Artist name: Consistent with existing catalog (spelling, capitalization)
[ ] Genre: Primary and secondary genre selected accurately
[ ] Composer credits: All contributors listed by legal name
[ ] ISRC: Will be assigned — verify in distribution dashboard post-submission
[ ] Release date: Minimum 7 days out (3–4 weeks if pitching to Spotify editorial)
[ ] PRO registration: Beat registered with ASCAP/BMI before release date
[ ] Publishing admin: Track submitted to Songtrust or equivalent for mechanical collection
[ ] Split sheet: Signed by all contributors before public release
[ ] Sample clearance: All samples cleared or original — documented
[ ] Content ID: Decision made — enroll or exclude, per licensing status of beat
```
