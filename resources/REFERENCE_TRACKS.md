# Reference Tracks for House Music Production and Mixing

Listen to these tracks while mixing and mastering. A/B your mix against them at matched volume levels.

---

## How to Use Reference Tracks

1. Import the track into your DAW on a dedicated reference track
2. Use a gain plugin to match loudness to your mix (-14 LUFS normalized)
3. Switch between your mix and the reference using the track mute button
4. Listen for: kick weight, low-end balance, high-end air, stereo width, dynamics, energy

## Mastering Reference Comparison

Use these tracks specifically for mastering reference — professional mixes that represent the genre's commercial target level:

| Track | Loudness Profile | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| "Cola" — CamelPhat | -8 to -9 LUFS | Tech house standard loudness |
| "Glue" — Bicep | -10 to -11 LUFS | Slightly more dynamic; Beatport focus |
| "We Dance Again" — Black Coffee | -11 LUFS | Slightly dynamic, organic feel |
| "Show Me Love" — Robin S (remaster) | -9 LUFS | Commercial remaster loudness |
| Any Defected Records release | -9 to -10 LUFS | Consistent label standard |

**Note on LUFS measurement:** Use a loudness meter plugin (SPAN, Youlean, or Melda MAnalyzer) to measure the LUFS of your reference tracks. Match your master to within 1 LUFS of your target reference.

## Quick A/B Checklist When Using References

When switching between your mix and a reference, check:

- [ ] **Low-end:** Does the reference sound more defined/clear below 100 Hz?
- [ ] **Kick punch:** Does the reference kick hit harder? (Often: yes, study why)
- [ ] **Midrange clarity:** Is the vocal/pad more present or more recessed in the reference?
- [ ] **High-end air:** Does the reference sound brighter (10–16 kHz)?
- [ ] **Stereo width:** Is the reference wider or narrower? Where does it differ?
- [ ] **Dynamic range:** Does the reference breathe more or less than your mix?
- [ ] **Overall energy:** Does the reference have more "forward" push and momentum?
