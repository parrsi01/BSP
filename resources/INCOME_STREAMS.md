## 1. Beat Leasing (Non-Exclusive)

**What it is:** An artist pays a one-time fee to use your beat for a limited purpose (e.g., mixtape, under 10,000 streams). The beat stays in your catalog and can be sold again to other artists.

**Typical pricing:**
- Basic lease (MP3): $15–$30
- Premium lease (WAV + trackout): $30–$75
- Unlimited lease: $100–$300

**How to start:**
1. Set up a BeatStars or Airbit storefront (both offer free tiers).
2. Upload beats with a tagged MP3 preview (your beat tag audible throughout).
3. Set license tiers and automate delivery via the platform.
4. Link your store in every SoundCloud description and Instagram bio.

**Revenue model:** Passive once set up. Volume is the key — 50+ beats in catalog is the real starting point for consistent income.

---

## 2. Exclusive Beat Sales

**What it is:** One artist pays a premium to be the sole user of a beat. The beat is removed from your store after sale.

**Typical pricing:** $100–$2,000 depending on your reputation, genre demand, and beat quality.

**How to start:**
1. List every beat on BeatStars with an exclusive price (typically 10–30x your lease price).
2. Offer a brief negotiation window via DM for serious buyers.
3. Use a written exclusive purchase agreement (see MUSIC_BUSINESS_101.md for outline).
4. Deliver: untagged WAV, all stems, and a signed contract.

**Key rule:** Never sell an exclusive on a beat that was already leased non-exclusively without disclosing this to the buyer. Most standard leases allow the producer to sell exclusive at any time, voiding existing leases — disclose this in your lease terms upfront.

---

## 3. Unlimited Lease

**What it is:** A middle tier between non-exclusive lease and exclusive. Buyer gets lifetime use on unlimited projects and distribution, but does not own the beat exclusively.

**Typical pricing:** $100–$300

**Setup:** Add as a tier on BeatStars between your WAV lease and exclusive. Delivers untagged WAV + stems. No stream caps, no project caps.

**Best for:** Artists with growing catalogs who want flexibility without the exclusive price.

---

## 4. Beat Bundles

**What it is:** 3–10 beats sold together at a discount (usually 30–50% off individual prices).

**Bundle structures:**
- Genre bundle: 5 Afrobeats beats for $75
- Tempo bundle: 5 beats at 140 BPM for artists in that niche
- Seasonal bundle: New Year pack, summer vibes, etc.

**How to start:**
1. On BeatStars, use the "Collection" feature to group beats with a bundle price.
2. On Gumroad, create a product with a ZIP file of multiple tagged MP3s.
3. Promote as a limited-time offer to create urgency.

**Key insight:** Bundles convert well because they lower cost-per-beat. A buyer hesitant to spend $30 on one beat will often pay $75 for five.

---

## 5. Beat Tapes

**What it is:** An album-format release of instrumentals, released under your producer name as a standalone project.

**Revenue paths:**
- Streaming royalties via DistroKid/CD Baby
- Direct sales on Bandcamp
- Collector vinyl (Bandcamp vinyl pressing for established acts)

**How to start:**
1. Curate 8–15 beats with a cohesive theme/sound.
2. Design album artwork (3000x3000px minimum).
3. Distribute via DistroKid ($22.99/yr unlimited).
4. Also publish to Bandcamp with a "name your price" or fixed price option.
5. Register the release with your PRO (ASCAP/BMI) for performance royalties.

**Realistic expectation:** $0–$50/month streaming in year one. Bandcamp pays out 85% (artist-friendly). Beat tapes build brand more than income early on.

## 6. Custom Beat Commissions

**What it is:** An artist pays you upfront to create a beat to their specific requirements (tempo, key, mood, reference track, instrumentation).

**Typical pricing:**
- Basic commission: $50–$150 (emerging producers)
- Mid-level: $200–$500 (established catalog, proven placements)
- Premium: $500–$2,000+ (notable credits, major label adjacent)

**How to start:**
1. Create a "Commission" page on your BeatStars or a simple Typeform with: BPM preference, key, mood, reference tracks, budget, deadline.
2. Require 50% deposit upfront, 50% on delivery.
3. Deliver 2 revisions max — define this in writing before starting.
4. Keep all rights until full payment cleared.

**Platform options:** Fiverr (high volume, lower prices), direct Instagram DMs (higher prices, more selective), SoundBetter (higher-end clientele).

## 7. Riddim Compilation

**What it is:** One beat with multiple vocalists recorded over it, released as an EP. Common in dancehall/reggaeton. Artists pay a fee to be featured.

**Revenue model:**
- Charge vocalists $50–$200 to be on the riddim
- Streaming royalties split between producer and vocalists per agreement
- Promotes all parties to each other's audiences

**How to start:**
1. Record a top-quality, radio-ready beat in a genre suited to this format.
2. Reach out to 8–12 vocalists via Instagram with a clear pitch.
3. Create a split sheet before anyone records.
4. Mix and master each vocal over the riddim.
5. Distribute as an EP under the riddim name (e.g., "Galaxy Riddim — Various Artists").
6. Register all tracks with your PRO.

## 8. Sample Pack / Drum Kit Sales

**What it is:** Selling a ZIP file of original, custom-made drum samples, one-shots, loops, or sound effects.

**Types:**
- Drum kits: kick drums, snares, hi-hats, claps, percs (100–500 samples)
- Loop packs: melodic loops at specific BPMs and keys
- One-shot packs: vocal chops, risers, FX hits

**Pricing:**
- Entry-level kit: $5–$15
- Premium named kit: $20–$60
- Bundle of kits: $40–$100

**Platforms:**
| Platform | Fee | Best For |
|----------|-----|---------|
| Gumroad | 10% + $0.30 | Quick setup, all file types |
| BeatStars | Included in plan | Producer audiences |
| Sellfy | $29/month flat | Store with multiple products |
| Direct (Shopify) | Transaction fees | Full brand control |

**How to start:** Build a kit from your own session samples. 100 sounds minimum. Tag every sample with metadata. Write a simple license (personal use + commercial use tiers).

**Key insight:** Drum kits have a lower price ceiling but near-zero marginal cost. One kit can sell 1,000 times with no additional work.

## 9. Preset Packs

**What it is:** Selling saved synth presets for Serum, Vital, Massive, Sylenth1, etc.

**Pricing:**
- Serum/Vital preset pack (50–150 presets): $10–$40
- Niche genre pack: $20–$60

**Requirements:** You must create original presets from scratch. Do not resell factory presets or presets you purchased — that violates most EULAs.

**How to start:**
1. Export presets from your DAW's synth as .fxp (Serum) or .vital (Vital) files.
2. Organize into categories: Leads, Basses, Pads, Arps, Plucks.
3. Create a demo track showing every preset in use.
4. List on Gumroad, BeatStars, or your own site.

**High-upside play:** Genre-specific presets (e.g., "House Music Serum Pack — 100 Presets") command premium prices because the audience is highly targeted.

## 10. Mixing & Mastering Services

**What it is:** Charging to mix and/or master other artists' tracks.

**Pricing:**
| Service | Entry | Mid | Pro |
|---------|-------|-----|-----|
| Mix only | $50 | $100–$200 | $300–$600 |
| Master only | $20 | $50–$80 | $100–$200 |
| Mix + Master | $75 | $150–$250 | $400–$800 |

**How to start:**
1. Build a portfolio of 5–10 before/after audio samples.
2. List on Fiverr with tiered packages.
3. Require the artist to send organized stems (no more than 30 tracks).
4. Establish a revision policy (2 revisions max per mix).

**Tools needed:** Calibrated reference monitors or studio headphones, professional metering plugins (LUFS meter, spectrum analyzer), experienced ears.

## 11. Ghost Production

**What it is:** You make beats/tracks credited to another artist or DJ. Common in EDM, but exists across genres. You are paid upfront and sign an NDA — your name never appears.

**Pricing:**
- Basic ghost beat: $200–$500
- Full ghost track (mix + master + stems): $500–$3,000
- Ongoing retainer (exclusive ghost producer): $2,000–$10,000/month

**How to start:**
1. Build a private portfolio (different from your public one).
2. Find clients through EDM producer forums, Discord communities, direct outreach.
3. Always use a written NDA and ghost production agreement (see MUSIC_BUSINESS_101.md).
4. Require full payment upfront or 50% deposit — no exceptions.

**Risk:** Your music will be promoted under someone else's name. You cannot take credit publicly. Consider carefully whether this aligns with your team's goals.

## 12. Music Lessons / Beat Making Coaching

**What it is:** Teaching 1-on-1 or group sessions on beat making, mixing, sound design, or music theory.

**Formats:**
| Format | Price Range | Time Investment |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1-on-1 Zoom session (1hr) | $50–$200 | High |
| Group workshop (live) | $20–$100/person | High |
| Recorded course (async) | $47–$197 one-time | Medium (build once) |
| Monthly coaching membership | $25–$100/month | Ongoing |

**Platforms:** Teachable, Kajabi, Zoom + Gumroad, Skool communities.

**How to start:** Define your niche (Afrohouse production, drum programming, mixing for rap). Create a free 5-minute sample lesson as your lead magnet. Post it to YouTube and IG with a booking link in bio.

## 13. YouTube AdSense

**What it is:** Monetizing YouTube videos through Google's ad program once you reach 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours (or 10M Shorts views).

**Estimated earnings:**
- Music producer channels: $1–$5 RPM (revenue per 1,000 views)
- Tutorial channels: $3–$8 RPM (higher advertiser value)
- 100,000 views/month = $100–$500/month

**Content that works:**
- Beat making videos (freestyle, time-lapse, challenge format)
- DAW tutorials
- Plugin reviews/comparisons
- "Making a beat from scratch" series

**How to start:**
1. Post consistently (2x/week minimum to grow).
2. Use keyword-optimized titles ("How to make Afrohouse beats in FL Studio").
3. Apply for monetization once thresholds hit.
4. Diversify: YouTube income alone is slow — use it to drive beat store traffic.

## 14. Fan-Powered Royalties (SoundCloud)

**What it is:** SoundCloud's royalty model where revenue comes from your actual fans' subscriptions, not a general pool. More fair for niche/loyal audiences.

**Requirements:** SoundCloud Pro Unlimited ($16/month) or free via SoundCloud for Artists.

**Estimated earnings:** $0.01–$0.04 per stream from paying fans. Small but grows with a dedicated SC fanbase.

**How to maximize:** Upload consistently, engage comments, repost community, use SoundCloud groups for genre visibility.

## 15. Streaming Royalties (Spotify/Apple Music)

**What it is:** Royalties generated when your beat tapes or instrumentals are streamed.

**Per-stream rates (approximate 2024):**
| Platform | Per Stream |
|----------|------------|
| Spotify | $0.003–$0.005 |
| Apple Music | $0.007–$0.01 |
| Tidal | $0.01–$0.013 |
| Amazon Music | $0.004–$0.006 |
| YouTube Music | $0.001–$0.003 |

**Setup:** Distribute via DistroKid, TuneCore, or CD Baby. Register compositions with a PRO to also collect performance royalties.

**Realistic expectation:** 10,000 streams/month = $30–$100. Streaming alone is not a primary income source at the producer level — treat it as a discovery tool and royalty supplement.

## 16. Sync Licensing

**What it is:** Licensing your music for use in TV shows, films, commercials, video games, YouTube videos, and podcasts. Highest per-placement earning potential.

**Typical fees:**
| Placement | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Local TV ad | $500–$2,000 |
| National TV ad | $5,000–$50,000+ |
| Film (indie) | $500–$5,000 |
| Film (major) | $10,000–$100,000+ |
| Video game | $500–$20,000 |
| YouTuber (sync) | $50–$500 |

**How to start:**
1. Create a catalog of production-ready instrumentals (fully mixed, no samples, cleared).
2. Submit to music libraries: Musicbed, Artlist, Musicbed, Soundsnap, Pond5.
3. Register every track with your PRO before submission.
4. Ensure you own or have cleared all samples — libraries will reject or blacklist you for uncleared samples.

**Critical requirement:** Beats used for sync must be sample-free or fully cleared. See SAMPLE_CLEARANCE.md.

## 17. Jingle Production

**What it is:** Creating short musical themes or full jingles for local businesses, podcasts, YouTube channels, and content creators.

**Pricing:**
- Local business jingle (15–30 seconds): $150–$500
- Podcast intro/outro: $50–$200
- YouTuber theme music: $100–$400
- Full commercial jingle: $300–$2,000+

**How to start:**
1. Create 3–5 jingle demos covering different styles (upbeat, corporate, fun/quirky).
2. List on Fiverr with clear packages and turnaround times.
3. Cold-pitch local businesses via email or Instagram (restaurants, barbershops, boutiques).
4. Offer a 3-second free preview mockup to close indecisive clients.

## 18. Podcast Music

**What it is:** Licensing background music and themed tracks for podcasters.

**Models:**
- One-time license: $20–$150 per track
- Subscription license: $15–$50/month (creator pays per month of use)
- Library deal: non-exclusive submission to podcast music libraries

**Platforms:** Epidemic Sound, Artlist, Musicbed (submission portals), direct sales via Gumroad.

**Key content:** Lo-fi loops, conversational background pads, genre-specific bumpers (true crime, business, comedy). Short, loopable formats preferred (30–60 second loops).

## 19. Stock Music Sites

**What it is:** Submitting your instrumentals to licensing libraries where content creators, filmmakers, and businesses can license them for a fee.

**Platforms:**
| Site | Revenue Model | Producer Cut |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Pond5 | Non-exclusive, set your price | 35–50% |
| AudioJungle (Envato) | Non-exclusive | 30–70% (tiered) |
| Musicbed | Curated, invitation | 50% |
| Artlist | Annual subscription model | Flat fee per track |
| Epidemic Sound | Staff/invites only | Salary + royalties |

**How to start:** Start with Pond5 (open submissions) and AudioJungle. Upload 20–50 tracks minimum to see meaningful income. Optimize titles and tags for search.

**Time to income:** 2–6 months to first sale. Growing a library of 200+ tracks is when stock music becomes meaningful passive income.

## 20. Affiliate Marketing

**What it is:** Earning commission by referring other producers to tools and platforms you use.

**High-value programs:**
| Company | Commission | Cookie Window |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| Splice | $5–$20 per sign-up | 30 days |
| BeatStars | 20% of referral's plan | 60 days |
| Plugin Boutique | 10–15% of sale | 30 days |
| Native Instruments | 8–10% | 30 days |
| Loopmasters | 15% | 30 days |

**How to start:**
1. Sign up for affiliate programs of tools you already use.
2. Add affiliate links to YouTube video descriptions, Instagram bio link aggregator (Linktree), and SoundCloud profiles.
3. Never promote tools you haven't personally used — your audience will notice.

**Realistic income:** $20–$500/month depending on audience size. Scales well with YouTube/tutorial content.

## 21. Producer Subscription / Patreon

**What it is:** Monthly supporters pay a recurring fee in exchange for exclusive beats, early access, behind-the-scenes content, or direct feedback.

**Tier structure example:**
| Tier | Price | Benefit |
|------|-------|---------|
| Supporter | $3/month | Early access to all beats |
| Producer | $10/month | 2 exclusive leases/month + Discord |
| Pro | $25/month | 5 leases + stems + monthly feedback session |

**Platforms:** Patreon, Ko-fi, BeatStars subscription feature.

**How to start:** Launch with a minimum of 3 tiers. Announce to existing SoundCloud/IG followers. The key is consistent monthly delivery — subscribers churn fast if you miss deliveries.

## 22. Live DJ Sets / Events

**What it is:** Getting booked to perform at clubs, events, festivals, or private functions.

**Rates:**
- Local club night: $100–$500
- Private event / wedding DJ: $300–$1,500
- Festival (emerging): $200–$1,000 + accommodation
- Residency: $200–$800/week

**How to start:**
1. Build a DJ set (even using your own beats + complementary tracks).
2. Record a 30–60 minute mix and upload to SoundCloud/Mixcloud.
3. Submit press kit to local venues: bio, photo, mix link, availability.
4. Network at events — most bookings come via word of mouth.

**Equipment needed:** Controller (Pioneer DDJ-400 or equivalent), laptop, DJ software (Rekordbox, Serato).

---

## 23. Producer Management

**What it is:** Managing other producers — handling their marketing, beat store, client acquisition, and business development — in exchange for a percentage of their revenue.

**Typical manager cut:** 15–25% of gross revenue.

**When to pursue:** Only once BSP has a proven marketing system, established contacts, and bandwidth. This is a relationship business — reputation is everything.

**How to start:**
1. Document your own team's systems and results.
2. Identify 1–2 talented solo producers with no business infrastructure.
3. Draft a management agreement (6–12 month term, 20% cut, defined deliverables).
4. Start with one client before scaling.

## Priority Tier for New Teams

**Start here (first 90 days):**
- Beat leasing (non-exclusive) — core
- Beat bundles — quick wins
- Sample pack / drum kit — high margin, low time

**Add at 6 months:**
- Custom commissions — direct client income
- YouTube tutorials — long-term compounding
- Patreon — recurring base

**12+ months:**
- Sync licensing — requires clean catalog
- Stock music libraries — requires volume
- Ghost production — requires reputation
