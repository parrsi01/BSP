# Artist Outreach — Finding Buyers and Building Relationships

---

## Where to Find Artists

### SoundCloud — [soundcloud.com](https://soundcloud.com)
The strongest producer-to-artist discovery channel.

**Search strategies:**
- Search your genre: "deep house vocalist", "afro house singer", "house music singer"
- Check who's commenting on popular house tracks — active commenters are active artists
- Follow artists who post originals (not just reposts)
- Look at who follows your target artists — they're all in the same ecosystem

**Signals that an artist is a good prospect:**
- Posting original music (not covers)
- Engaging with other producers
- Bio mentions "looking for beats" or "available for features"
- Consistent upload history (they're active)

---

### TikTok
- Search: #housesinger, #producersearch, #needbeats, #vocalistforsale
- Artists who post acapella or freestyle TikToks are actively looking for placements
- Artists posting "I need a producer" content are direct opportunities

### Instagram
- Hashtags: #housevocalist, #housemusicsinger, #needproducer, #singeravailable
- Look at followers of major house labels (Defected, Toolroom, Anjunadeep)
- Many emerging artists will be in the "following" lists of accounts they admire

### SubmitHub — [submithub.com](https://www.submithub.com)
- Primarily for artists submitting to blogs/playlists, but useful for identifying active, serious artists
- Search artists in the house genre who are clearly investing in their music career

### Direct Search Approach
- Search "[genre] singer looking for producer" on Google, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter
- These are self-identified prospects — the most qualified

## Cold DM Templates That Actually Get Replies

The single biggest mistake in artist outreach: leading with the sell. Lead with value or genuine connection.

### Template 1 — "I Made Something for You"
```
Hey [Name], been following your music for a bit — your voice has
a really unique tone that would sit well over house music.
I produce house beats and made something that reminded me of
your vibe. Want me to send it over? No strings attached.
```
**Why it works:** Personal, low-pressure, gives something first.

---

### Template 2 — After Listening to Their Music
```
Hey [Name], listened to "[specific track title]" — the
[specific element: vocal run, delivery on the bridge] hit
different. We produce house music and think your voice would
work really well on what we're building. Want to hear a beat?
```
**Why it works:** Shows you actually listened. Specific compliments signal authenticity.

---

### Template 3 — Responding to a "Need Beats" Post
```
Hey! Saw you're looking for beats — we produce house music
(deep / tech / afro). Here's a 30-second clip of something
recent: [link]. If this vibe works for you, we can send the
full beat free to see if it fits. What's your project about?
```
**Why it works:** Direct response to their stated need. Immediate value (the clip).

---

### Template 4 — Collab Offer
```
Hey [Name], big fan of "[track]." We're BSP — 4 producers
focused on house. We're working on a project and think your
voice would be a great fit for one of the tracks. Would you
be open to a collab? We handle production, you own your
vocals — we'd split the release equally.
```
**Why it works:** Clearly defines the value proposition. Equal partnership framing.

---

### DM Length Rule
Keep it under 4 sentences. Long DMs from strangers get skimmed or ignored. One compliment, one offer, one question.

## Free Beat Offer Strategy to Build Relationships

The "free beat" strategy:

1. **Select a beat** that you've heard the artist could work with (based on their existing music)
2. DM with the clip and offer the full untagged version free
3. **Condition:** "Just tag me in anything you release with it — [your handle]"
4. When they release it: repost, comment, engage — public reciprocity

### Why This Works
- It costs you nothing if the beat wasn't going to sell anyway
- The artist gets value → positive association with your brand
- When they release something, you both benefit from the exposure
- It opens a relationship that can lead to paid work later

### Frequency
Offer free beats to 3–5 artists per week. Even if only 1 in 5 uses it, you've created 1 new relationship and 1 new release per week.

## How to Vet an Artist Before Giving Exclusive Rights

Before agreeing to an exclusive license (you can no longer sell the beat):

### Checklist — Minimum for Exclusive Consideration

| Check | Acceptable | Caution |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Social following | 5,000+ genuine followers | Under 500 — limited upside |
| Content consistency | Posts regularly, active | Inactive 2+ months |
| Music quality | Professional recordings | Home recording only |
| Engagement rate | 2–5%+ on posts | Under 0.5% on every post |
| Past releases | Has actual released music | Only demo content |
| Distribution | Using DistroKid/CD Baby/etc | Never released to Spotify |

### Red Flags — Decline Exclusive
- Wants exclusive but can't explain what they plan to do with the beat
- No released music anywhere
- Offers to pay "after the release makes money"
- Follower count looks inflated (many followers but minimal engagement)
- No verifiable identity (new account, no bio, no profile photo)

## Collab vs License: When to Propose Each

| Situation | Propose | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Artist has < 10K followers | Non-exclusive lease | Limited upside; keep the beat available |
| Artist is actively building and releasing | Non-exclusive or unlimited | Support their growth, maintain flexibility |
| Artist has 50K+ followers or label support | Exclusive option | Upside justifies removing from catalog |
| Collab with features (both parties investing) | Collab split | Both invest; split the release |
| Artist wants full ownership | Exclusive only | Price it appropriately; not under $300 |

## Following Up Without Being Annoying

### The 3-Touch Rule
Reach out a maximum of 3 times on any single conversation thread:
1. Initial DM
2. Follow-up after 5–7 days if no response: "Hey, just wanted to make sure you saw this. Sending a second clip if you want"
3. Final follow-up 14 days later: short and light

After 3 unanswered messages: **stop**. Move on. Don't pressure or guilt-trip.

### Reopening Conversations
After 1–2 months of silence, it's acceptable to reconnect with new content:
- "Hey, just dropped something new — thought of your sound immediately: [clip]"
- No reference to previous unanswered DMs

## Building a Client List and CRM

As your outreach scales, tracking becomes essential. Use **Notion** or **Airtable** (both free tiers):

### Spreadsheet/Database Fields

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Artist Name | Stage name |
| Platform | Instagram / SoundCloud / TikTok |
| Profile URL | Link to their profile |
| Genre | What they make |
| Followers | Approximate following |
| First Contact Date | When you first DM'd |
| Last Contact | Most recent interaction |
| Status | Cold / Contacted / Replied / Active / Client / Inactive |
| Beat Sent | Name of free beat sent (if any) |
| Notes | Anything relevant |
| Purchases | List any paid transactions |

### Status Definitions
- **Cold:** You know of them, haven't reached out yet
- **Contacted:** You sent a DM, no response
- **Replied:** They responded (even "not interested")
- **Active:** Ongoing conversation or they downloaded a free beat
- **Client:** Has purchased a license
- **Inactive:** Was active, gone quiet for 30+ days

## Artist → Long-Term Client Conversion Strategy

Converting a one-time buyer to a repeat client is 5x easier than finding a new client.

### After a Purchase
1. **Deliver immediately** and with a personal touch: "Just sent you the files — let me know if you need anything adjusted"
2. **Check in 2 weeks later:** "Did you get the session laid down? Would love to hear what you did with it"
3. **When they release:** React publicly, share on your stories, comment on the post

### The Next Beat Offer
After they've released something with your beat:
- "So glad that came together well — I actually just finished something that has a similar energy to [their track]. Want the first listen?"
- This "first listen" framing creates exclusivity and keeps them engaged

### Client VIP Treatment
For clients who've bought 2+ times:
- Early access to new beats before public drop
- Occasional discount codes
- Option to lock in current pricing before your next price increase

### Long-Term Relationship Signals
An artist who becomes a regular client will often:
- Tag you unprompted in social posts
- Send other artists your way (referrals)
- Buy higher-tier licenses as their budget grows
- Work with you on custom beats (highest revenue per engagement)

**Custom beats** (produced specifically for one artist at their direction) typically command $200–$1,000+ depending on the artist's level and your reputation. Your best long-term clients will eventually want customs.
