# Email Marketing for Beat Sellers

Email is your highest-converting sales channel. An artist who gave you their email address is worth 10x more than a social media follower.

---

## Why Email Beats Social Media for Direct Sales

| Factor | Email List | Instagram/TikTok |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Reach | 20–40% open rate | 2–8% of followers see posts |
| Control | You own the list | Platform can delete your account |
| Revenue per 1,000 | $30–$50+ per send | $1–$5 per post |
| Algorithm | None — direct delivery | Constantly changes |
| Relationship depth | High — personal inbox | Low — scroll-and-pass |

**Rule:** Build the email list before you need it.

## Free Beat for Email Signup Funnel

The most effective way to build a beat producer email list:

### The Funnel Structure
```
Social media post (beat clip)
  → "Link in bio for free beat"
  → Landing page (Mailchimp/ConvertKit)
  → Enter email to download
  → Confirmation email with download link
  → Welcome sequence begins
```

### Setting Up the Free Beat Funnel

**Step 1: Pick the free beat**
- Your best non-exclusive beat that you no longer plan to sell
- Or a beat specifically made as a "lead magnet" — high quality, unique to the funnel
- WAV quality (not tagged) — the value must justify giving an email address

**Step 2: Create the landing page**
- Mailchimp: use their free landing page builder
- ConvertKit: "Landing Pages" in the free tier
- Include: beat name, genre, BPM, embedded audio player or short preview clip
- Headline: "Free [Genre] Beat — High Quality WAV, No Tag, Ready to Record"
- One input field: email address only

**Step 3: Delivery email**
Send immediately on signup:
- Subject: "Your free beat is here 🎵"
- Body: 2 sentences + download button + "Here's what's coming to your inbox" (tease the sequence)
- Attach or link the WAV (Mailchimp: use a hosted file link; ConvertKit: use a file download block)

## Welcome Sequence — 5-Email Series Template

Automated emails sent after signup. Space them: Day 1, Day 3, Day 5, Day 8, Day 14.

### Email 1 — Instant (Free Beat Delivery)
- **Subject:** "Your free [genre] beat is here"
- **Body:** Download link + 2 sentences about BSP + set expectations ("I drop new beats weekly")
- **CTA:** Download the beat

### Email 2 — Day 3
- **Subject:** "What producers don't tell you about house beats"
- **Body:** One genuine tip about making house music or choosing the right beat. Position yourself as knowledgeable, not just a salesperson.
- **CTA:** Check out more beats on BeatStars (soft link, not hard sell)

### Email 3 — Day 5
- **Subject:** "New beat just dropped — want it?"
- **Body:** Introduce one specific beat from your catalog. Embed audio or link. Describe the vibe in 2–3 sentences. Price clearly.
- **CTA:** "Grab it on BeatStars → [link]"

### Email 4 — Day 8
- **Subject:** "The artists who used our beats this week"
- **Body:** Social proof. If you have artists who've used your beats, name-drop (with permission). If you're new, share a test recording over your beat.
- **CTA:** "See the full catalog"

### Email 5 — Day 14
- **Subject:** "Subscriber exclusive — 20% off this week only"
- **Body:** A discount code (set in BeatStars/Airbit) for subscribers only. Time-limited (expires in 72 hours). This email exists to convert fence-sitters.
- **CTA:** "Use code BSP20 at checkout"

## Ongoing Beat Drop Announcement Emails

After the welcome sequence, shift to a regular broadcast rhythm:

### Weekly Beat Drop Email Structure
- **Subject:** "[NEW] Deep House Beat - "[Beat Name]" | 126 BPM"
- **Preview:** Embedded audio widget (SoundCloud or BeatStars widget code)
- **Body:** 3–4 sentences on the vibe, inspiration, BPM/key info
- **CTA 1:** Buy on BeatStars (primary CTA)
- **CTA 2:** Reply to this email if you want a free version (relationship building)
- **Footer:** Unsubscribe link (required by CAN-SPAM), social links

## Mailchimp vs ConvertKit

### Mailchimp — [mailchimp.com](https://mailchimp.com)
| Feature | Free Tier | Paid |
|---------|----------|------|
| Contacts | Up to 500 | Paid plans for more |
| Monthly sends | 1,000 | More with paid |
| Automation | Basic | Advanced |
| Landing pages | Yes | Yes |
| Best for | Starting out | N/A |

### ConvertKit — [convertkit.com](https://convertkit.com)
| Feature | Free Tier | Paid |
|---------|----------|------|
| Contacts | Up to 1,000 | $9/month+ for more |
| Monthly sends | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Automation | Full automation | Full automation |
| Landing pages | Yes | Yes |
| Best for | Creators and digital products | Scaling up |

**Recommendation:** Start with **ConvertKit free** — better automation on the free tier for this use case (free beat funnel). Move to paid when you exceed 1,000 subscribers.

## Subject Line Templates That Convert

The subject line determines whether the email gets opened (30–50% open rates are achievable with good subject lines).

### Beat Drop Subject Lines
- `[NEW BEAT] Deep House - "Midnight Drive" | 126 BPM`
- `Just dropped a banger. Want it?`
- `The beat I made at 3am [listen]`
- `[EXCLUSIVE] 24 hours only — deep house beat drop`
- `Produced this specifically for [their genre] artists`

### Promotional Subject Lines
- `Subscriber discount: 20% off expires Friday`
- `5 beats, 1 price — this weekend only`
- `Free beat inside (no strings attached)`
- `[YOUR NAME] x BSP — new collab beats available`

### Relationship/Value Subject Lines
- `How we mixed the kick in our last drop (tutorial)`
- `3 free loop packs for house producers`
- `What we're working on this month`

### Rules for Subject Lines
1. Keep under 50 characters (mobile display)
2. No all-caps (spam filters and looks aggressive)
3. Test emojis — sometimes they boost open rates, sometimes not (A/B test)
4. Personalization token `{{first_name}}` in subject increases open rates 10–15%

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| Open rate | 25–40% | Subject line quality |
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 3–8% | Email body + CTA quality |
| Conversion rate | 1–5% | Offer quality and pricing |
| Unsubscribe rate | <0.5% per email | Content relevance |
| List growth rate | 10+ new/week | Funnel performance |
| Revenue per email | Track total | Overall list value |

### Monthly Review Questions
- Which email had the highest open rate? Why? (Steal the subject line formula)
- Which email drove the most BeatStars clicks?
- What % of purchases came from email vs social?
- Did the discount email convert more than the standard drops?
