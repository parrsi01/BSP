## 1. Account Type — Creator vs Business

| Feature | Creator Account | Business Account |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Insights/analytics | Full access | Full access |
| Promoted posts (ads) | Yes | Yes |
| Instagram Shopping | Limited | Yes |
| Primary category label | Music/Artist options | Industry options |
| Contact button types | Book Now, Email, etc. | More options |
| Link in bio | One link (use Linktree) | One link (use Linktree) |
| Story link sticker | Yes (all accounts now) | Yes |
| Scheduling via Meta Suite | Yes | Yes |

**Verdict: Creator Account.** The "Musician" or "Music Producer" category label appears under your name on your profile, which adds legitimacy. Business accounts are better suited for storefronts selling physical goods. Both get the same analytics and ad access — creator wins for producers.

[Genre] producer

Character limit is 150 for the bio. Every character counts. No filler.

Instagram Highlights are the circles above your feed. They persist permanently and are often the first thing a new profile visitor taps.

Recommended highlights and what to put in each:

| Highlight name | Content |

Create branded covers for each Highlight using Canva — same color palette as your BeatStars banner.

## 4. Reels Strategy

Reels are Instagram's highest-reach format. A Reel reaches non-followers; a feed post reaches mostly your existing audience. For growth, Reels are the engine.

### The hook — first 1 second

The algorithm measures if people keep watching past 3 seconds. A hook that fails in the first second kills the video's reach. For beat clips:

- Start with the beat already playing at full intensity — no silence, no title card
- OR start with a visual action (a hand hitting a pad, the waveform peaking, a screen showing the drop point)
- Do not start with your logo, a name tag, or any static image — movement retains attention

### Optimal Reel length for beats
- **15–30 seconds:** Best for pure beat clips — captures the drop and outro, nothing more
- **30–60 seconds:** Better for process content — enough time to show the arc of building a beat
- **60–90 seconds:** Tutorial content or stories — viewer opted in for depth

For beat drops: 15–30 seconds is the sweet spot. Most buyers decide in the first 10 seconds whether they want to hear more.

### Text overlay formulas that drive saves
Saves are a strong algorithm signal — they indicate the content is valuable enough to return to.

- `[Genre] | [BPM] BPM | [Key] | For sale — link in bio` (saves for reference when they're ready to buy)
- `Use this beat for free — grab it on SoundCloud (link in bio)` (saves to come back to it)
- `How I [achieved result] using [technique]` on educational content (saves the tip)

Keep text minimal — 3–5 words max on any single overlay. Use CapCut for text animations that appear in sync with the beat's rhythm.

### Audio selection
Two approaches, both valid:

1. **Use your beat as the audio:** Best for building brand recognition around your sound. The beat itself becomes discoverable as a TikTok/IG sound.
2. **Layer a trending audio:** Instagram allows you to play trending audio at low volume and mix your beat on top. This can boost reach because your Reel appears in the trending audio's discovery feed.

In the first 90 days, use your own beat as the primary audio consistently. Once your profile has momentum, experiment with trending audio layering.

### Caption length and CTA

| Caption type | Length | Best for |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Minimal ("This beat.") | 1–5 words | Strong visual content that speaks for itself |
| Standard | 1–3 sentences + hashtags | Most beat clips |
| Extended | 5–10 sentences | Educational or storytelling content |

Every caption needs one CTA. Options:
- "Link in bio → full catalog"
- "Comment 'FREE' for the download link"
- "DM me for custom beats"
- "Save this for your next session"

Use only one CTA per post — multiple CTAs reduce conversion rate.

## 5. Story Strategy

Stories disappear after 24 hours but appear at the top of your followers' feeds. They are the best tool for daily presence without the pressure of a permanent post.

### Daily Stories cadence

| Story type | When to post | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| "Good morning" beat snippet | Morning | Daily presence |
| Work in progress clip | Afternoon | Behind the scenes |
| Engagement poll or question | Evening | Algorithm signal + feedback |
| Beat drop link sticker | On drop day | Direct conversion |
| Repost of an artist using your beat | When available | Social proof |

3–5 Stories per day is sustainable. Under 3 = you disappear from the feed. Over 10 = dilutes attention.

### High-performing Story formats

**Polls:**
- "Which beat should I drop this Friday: A or B?" — plays 5 seconds of each
- "Rate this beat: fire or skip?" — binary choice increases response rate
- "Should I drop this one as a free download?" — makes followers feel they have power

**Countdown sticker:**
- Create a countdown called "New beat dropping" set to your planned release time
- Followers can subscribe to get a notification when it goes off
- This is the most direct drop announcement tool available

**Question sticker:**
- "What genre do you want next?" — content research + engagement
- "Want this beat free?" — generates DMs you can use to build your list
- "What's missing from your catalog as an artist?" — insight into artist pain points

**Link sticker:**
- Instagram Link sticker is now available to all accounts (no follower minimum)
- Use it every time you have a specific beat to sell — link directly to that beat's BeatStars page, not just your profile homepage
- Works far better than "link in bio" friction

## 6. Hashtag Research

### Finding active hashtags for house and trap dancehall

Method:
1. Search each hashtag in Instagram
2. Look at the "Recent" tab — not "Top"
3. If recent posts are from today or yesterday, the hashtag is active
4. If the most recent post is weeks old, the hashtag is dead for your niche

### Recommended hashtag sets

**For house beats:**
```
#housemusic #deephouse #techhouse #afrohouse
#housebeats #houseproducer #deephousemusic
#beatsforsale #beatstars #musicproducer
#flstudio #abletonlive #electronicmusic
#clubmusic #dancemusic #underground
#beatmaker #producerlife #housebeatselling
#deephouselove #techhouselove
```

**For trap dancehall:**
```
#dancehall #dancehallmusic #trapdancehall
#dancehallbeats #jamaican #reggaeton
#beatsforsale #beatstars #musicproducer
#riddim #dancehallproducer #afrobeats

```

Use 20–25 hashtags total. Mix one large (5M+ posts), several medium (500K–5M), and several niche (10K–500K). The niche ones actually drive targeted traffic.

## 7. Engagement Pods

An engagement pod is a private group of producers who all engage with each other's new posts immediately after publishing. Early engagement (within the first hour) signals to Instagram that the post is worth showing to more people.

### Setting one up (free method)
1. Find 8–12 other producers in your genre on Instagram (not direct competitors selling the exact same sound — complementary producers)
2. Create a WhatsApp or Telegram group
3. When anyone posts, they share the link in the group
4. Everyone else likes, comments meaningfully, and saves within 30 minutes
5. Rotate — everyone benefits equally

### Pod rules that make it work
- Genuine comments only (not "fire bro") — Instagram detects engagement quality
- No more than 15 people — larger pods become unmanageable and dilute the personal engagement
- Post the link as soon as you publish — don't wait hours

## 8. DM Strategy — Artist Outreach

Cold DMing artists with "buy my beat" is the fastest way to get ignored. The approach below works because it earns trust before asking for anything.

### The engagement-first approach

1. Find 20 target artists per week (house singers, rappers, vocalists in your subgenre)
2. For 5–7 days: like their last 3 posts, leave one genuine comment per post (not "fire bro" — something specific about the content)
3. After you have shown up 3+ times in their notifications, send a DM:

**First DM script:**
```
Hey [Name] — been following your music for a bit, really liked [specific song/video].

I make [genre] beats and I actually have one that reminds me of your style.
Would you want to hear it? No strings attached, just curious what you think.
```

4. If they respond positively, send the beat as an unlisted SoundCloud link or a BeatStars preview link — never send an unsolicited file attachment

5. Follow up once only if no response after 5 days. If still no response — move on.

### The post-engagement DM
After an artist comments on your Reel or views your Story multiple times, they have shown interest. DM them:

```
Hey [Name] — noticed you've been tuning in.
Got a [genre] beat I think would actually suit you.
Want me to send it over?
```

This feels like a personal invitation, not spam.

## 9. Collab Posts with Artists

Instagram Collab posts appear on both profiles simultaneously — your followers and the artist's followers both see it.

**How to set up:**
1. Create the Reel or feed post as normal
2. Before posting, tap "Tag People" → "Invite Collaborator"
3. Enter the artist's Instagram username
4. They receive a request to accept — once accepted, the post shows on both profiles

**What to collab on:**
- Artist posting a vocal or verse over your beat
- Joint studio session clip
- Artist reacting to hearing your beat for the first time (genuine, unscripted reactions are compelling)

Target artists with 2K–50K followers for collabs — big enough to extend your reach meaningfully, small enough to say yes to a collab with a new producer.

## 10. Instagram SEO

Instagram has search functionality that uses keywords — not just hashtags. Optimizing for it is a free, passive discovery method.

### Keyword placement

| Location | What to put | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Name field (edit profile) | Your genre + "beats" or "producer" | "BSP — Deep House Producer" |
| Bio | Genre keywords naturally | "Deep house & trap dancehall beats for sale" |
| Caption keywords | Include the genre name spelled out in the first sentence | "New deep house beat dropping tonight..." |
| Alt text on images | Describe the content with keywords | "Deep house beat waveform visualizer, 124 BPM" |

### How to add alt text
Edit post → Advanced Settings → Write Alt Text. Most producers skip this — it is free SEO you are leaving on the table.

## 11. Free Growth Methods

### Comment on 20 target accounts daily
Find your target artists (people who would buy beats) and leave one real comment per day on 20 of their posts. Not beat promotion — just genuine engagement. Over 30 days this creates 600 touch points with potential buyers. A percentage will check your profile.

### Follow/unfollow (ethical approach)
The ethics issue with follow/unfollow is when done in bulk with bots. The ethical version:
1. Follow 20 relevant accounts per day (artists in your genre, producers, music blogs)
2. Engage genuinely for 2 weeks
3. If they don't follow back and you have no relationship, unfollow
4. Keep your followers/following ratio reasonable (avoid 500 following, 50 followers)
5. Do this manually, never with a bot — Instagram bans bot accounts aggressively

### Share to Stories from other accounts' Reels
When a house music artist or related account posts a Reel, share it to your Story with a short comment. This shows up in that creator's notifications, creates goodwill, and keeps your Stories active with no content creation effort.

### Cross-post YouTube Shorts as Reels
Any short-form video content you create for YouTube Shorts can be posted as an Instagram Reel. One piece of content, two platform exposures. Use the same file — just re-upload via Instagram. The algorithm treats it as original content even if the same video exists on YouTube.
