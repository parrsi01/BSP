# TikTok Beat Marketing — Full Playbook

---

## 1. Why TikTok Works for Beat Sellers

TikTok's algorithm does not require a following to reach people. A new account with zero followers can get 50,000 views on its first video. This is categorically different from Instagram or YouTube, where follower count heavily influences reach.

For beat sellers specifically:
- The music production niche has a dedicated audience that watches "how it's made" content obsessively
- Beat drops perform well as short clips — the format matches the platform's optimal length
- Artists who need beats are actively on TikTok — you are in their feed
- Going viral on one beat video can drive enough BeatStars traffic to fund weeks of income

The catch: content frequency matters more than quality of any single video. Volume + consistency beats perfection.

## 2. Account Setup

### Username
Use your producer name, exactly as it appears on BeatStars and SoundCloud. Consistency across platforms is a brand asset. If your name is taken, add "music" or "beats" as a suffix — not numbers, which look like a burner account.

### Bio formula
```
[Genre] Producer 🎧
[City or "Worldwide"]
Beats for sale ↓
[Linktree URL]
```

Keep it under 80 characters. The link must be Linktree or a similar landing page — TikTok only allows one clickable link, so Linktree lets you point to BeatStars, SoundCloud, Instagram, and YouTube from one tap.

### Profile photo
Same image used on BeatStars and Instagram. Brand recognition compounds — when someone sees your face or logo across platforms, trust builds faster.

### Link in bio
Set up a Linktree (free tier is sufficient) with this button order:
1. "Buy My Beats" → BeatStars profile
2. "Free Beats" → SoundCloud profile
3. "Instagram" → Instagram profile
4. "YouTube" → YouTube channel

## 3. Content Types That Work for Beat Sellers

### Beat making process (sped up, 30–60 seconds)
Screen record your DAW session at normal speed, then speed it up 4–8x in CapCut. Show:
- Starting from empty project
- First drum pattern going in
- Adding a bass line
- The melody building
- Final version playing

Add your beat as the audio. Add minimal text: "Making a [genre] beat from scratch." This format gets strong watch time because it's visually dynamic and musically satisfying.

### "I made a beat in 60 seconds" format
A time challenge format. Real-time, not sped up. Open FL Studio, click record, make something in 60 seconds. The constraint is the hook — viewers watch to see if you can actually do it and what comes out. Results are often rougher but more authentic.

### Beat reveal (dramatic buildup then drop)
Build suspense. Start with 5 seconds of silence or ambient sound, then the beat kicks in hard at the drop. No intro text — let the music hit first. Text appears only after the beat drops: "This beat is for sale — link in bio." Works best when the beat has an actual moment of impact.

### Freestyle over your beat (invite viewers to rap/sing)
Post a 30-second clip of your beat and end with: "Who's rapping over this? Drop it in the comments." This generates comments (algorithm signal) and occasionally a creator will actually do it, giving you a duet opportunity.

### Before/after (raw vs mixed beat)
Split screen or sequential: the rough, unpolished version first, then the mixed, mastered version. Producers love this. Shows craft and the value of mixing/mastering. Good educational hook.

### "Rating my old beats" (nostalgia + self-roast)
Pull up beats from 1–3 years ago. React honestly. Self-aware criticism ("the drums were terrible") is funny and relatable. This humanizes you and builds connection. Artists buy from producers they feel they know.

### Studio setup tours
30-second walkthroughs of your setup — gear, monitors, interface, DAW layout. Setup content performs consistently across the producer community. Even a modest setup is interesting to aspiring producers.

### Responding to comments with a new beat
Pin a comment on one of your videos asking "what genre should I make next?" Then make a video responding to the best suggestion. Opens with: "You asked for [genre] — here it is." This creates a content series and rewards engaged followers.

## 4. Posting Frequency and Timing

### Frequency
- **Minimum:** 1 video per day to see meaningful growth
- **Optimal:** 2 videos per day (one beat clip, one process/educational)
- **Warning:** Do not post 5+ times per day — TikTok throttles accounts that post too frequently as spam signals

### Timing (general — calibrate to your analytics)

| Time slot | Why it works |
|-----------|-------------|
| 7–9 AM | Morning commute scroll |
| 12–2 PM | Lunch break |
| 7–10 PM | Peak evening usage globally |

After 30 days, check your TikTok analytics → Followers → "Most active times." This overrides any general advice — your actual audience data is the ground truth.

### Day-of-week patterns
Tuesday through Thursday tend to have higher engagement than weekends for music production content. Post your best content mid-week.

## 5. Hashtag Strategy

TikTok hashtags drive discoverability. Use 3–5 targeted hashtags — not 20. The algorithm reads hashtags to categorize your video, so precision beats volume.

### Core hashtags for beat sellers

| Category | Hashtags |
|----------|---------|
| General producer | #beatmaker #musicproducer #producerlife |
| Beat selling | #beatsforsale #buymybeats #beatstars |
| House music | #housemusic #deephouse #techhouse #afrohouse |
| Trap dancehall | #trapdancehall #dancehall #dancehallbeats |
| Process content | #flstudio #ableton #beatmaking #fromscratchtofinal |
| Discovery | #newmusic #undergroundmusic #independentartist |

### Hashtag research method
Search each hashtag in TikTok. Look at view counts on recent videos (not total view count — recent). Hashtags with 100M+ posts are competitive. Hashtags with 1M–50M are the sweet spot. Include one large, one medium, one niche per video.

## 6. The "Sound" Strategy

When you upload a video to TikTok using your beat as the audio, TikTok creates a "sound" — a public audio clip others can use in their own videos.

**How to maximize this:**
- When uploading your beat video, let TikTok use the original audio (do not mute or overlay a different sound)
- The sound becomes searchable in TikTok's audio library
- If another creator uses your sound, their video links back to your original, driving profile visits
- A beat that goes viral as a TikTok sound can generate thousands of plays and BeatStars traffic with zero additional effort

**Optimization:** Keep the most catchy 15 seconds of your beat as the opening of your TikTok video. This is the clip TikTok captures as the "sound preview" — make those 15 seconds the part that hooks people immediately.

## 7. Duet Strategy

TikTok's Duet feature splits the screen — your video on one side, another creator's video on the other, playing simultaneously.

**For beat sellers:**
1. Search TikTok for artists posting acapella vocals, freestyles, or singing without a beat (search: #acapella, #freestylerap, #singingsession)
2. Duet their video with your beat playing underneath
3. Caption: "Put a beat on it — [genre] vibes. Beat for sale, link in bio."
4. Tag the original creator in your caption
5. The creator often reshares your duet to their audience — free exposure

This works because you are solving a problem (they have a voice, you have a beat) and creating content that neither of you could make alone.

## 8. Collab with Vocalists — TikTok Collab Feature

TikTok's native Collab feature (separate from Duet — Collab is invited, Duet is one-sided) allows two creators to jointly post a video.

**Workflow:**
1. Identify a vocalist or rapper on TikTok with 1K–50K followers in your genre
2. Engage with their content for 1–2 weeks (likes, genuine comments)
3. DM: "Made something I think suits your style — want to do a Collab post?"
4. Record your side (beat playing, you in frame or DAW on screen), they record theirs
5. Post as a Collab — appears on both profiles, exposed to both audiences

The sweet spot is vocalists slightly larger than you — their audience benefits from the new content, yours grows from the exposure.

## 9. TikTok to SoundCloud Pipeline

Most TikTok viewers will not immediately buy a beat. The conversion to SoundCloud follower is a lower-commitment step that builds toward a purchase later.

**Pipeline:**
1. TikTok video caption: "Full version + free download on SoundCloud — link in bio"
2. Linktree "Free Beats" button → SoundCloud profile
3. SoundCloud free download requires follow + like → they become SC followers
4. SC followers hear future free drops → BeatStars link in SC descriptions → purchase

The SC follow is a micro-commitment. Buyers are far more likely to purchase from someone they already follow than from a stranger's TikTok profile.

## 10. TikTok to BeatStars Pipeline

For viewers who are ready to buy directly:

**Pipeline:**
1. Beat reveal video with strong drop moment
2. Caption: "This beat is available now — link in bio"
3. Linktree "Buy My Beats" button → BeatStars profile
4. BeatStars search or browse → find the specific beat from the video (match the title/thumbnail)
5. Purchase

**Friction reduction:** Make the BeatStars beat easy to find from TikTok. Use the same beat name and cover art in both places. If someone sees "Harbour Lights" in your TikTok, they should find it immediately when they land on BeatStars — no hunting.

## 11. TikTok Ads (Low Budget)

You do not need ads to grow. But a $20–$50 boost on a performing organic video can accelerate reach significantly.

### When to run a boost
Only boost a video that is already performing organically (500+ views in the first 24 hours). Boosting a weak video wastes money.

### Setup
- TikTok Ads Manager → Promote (the simpler option for individual videos)
- Objective: Profile visits or Website visits (to BeatStars)
- Audience: let TikTok auto-target based on the video content initially; after 3+ campaigns, narrow to music, hip-hop, electronic music interests
- Budget: $10–$20/day, 3-day campaign
- Measure: cost per link click to BeatStars; aim for under $0.50 per click

### What to boost
- A beat reveal with a strong drop moment
- A process video with strong watch-through rate (over 50%)
- Any video already getting unsolicited comments asking "where can I buy this?"

## 12. Analytics — What Metrics Matter

### Primary metrics for beat sellers

| Metric | What it means | Action if low |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Views per video | Raw reach | Change posting time or hook format |
| Watch time % | How much viewers watch | Fix the first 3 seconds — hook is weak |
| Profile visits | Viewers interested enough to check you out | Strong signal — content is working |
| Follows from video | Content converting to audience | Test different CTAs at end of video |
| Link clicks (Linktree) | Direct traffic to BeatStars/SC | Weak CTA in caption or bio |

### What to ignore early
- Likes count — vanity metric; watch time and profile visits matter more
- Shares — nice but not within your control
- Comments — valuable for engagement signal but not a direct revenue indicator

Check analytics weekly. Identify your top 3 performing videos each week and note what they have in common — format, topic, audio, posting time. Double down on those patterns.
