## Section 1: The Zero-Budget Stack

Every tool in this stack is free. Set up all of these before spending a single rand/dollar on marketing.

### Beat stores (free tiers)

| Tool | Free tier limits | What to do |
|------|----------------|-----------|
| BeatStars | 10 beat uploads, higher commission | Start here — get the profile right, upload first 10 best beats |
| Airbit | 10 beat uploads, embeddable player | Mirror BeatStars catalog here; use the embed widget |
| Gumroad | 10% fee, unlimited products | Use for beat bundles and sample packs as a secondary option |

### Website and landing pages

| Tool | Free tier | Use for |
|------|----------|---------|
| Carrd.co | 3-page sites on free plan | Simple producer website with BeatStars embed and social links |
| Linktree | Unlimited links, basic analytics | Bio link for Instagram, TikTok, Twitter — button grid pointing to all platforms |

### Social platforms (all free)

| Platform | Account type | Priority |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Instagram | Creator account | Primary visual platform |
| TikTok | Standard account | Primary discovery platform |
| YouTube | Standard channel | SEO and long-form |
| SoundCloud | Free artist account | Producer-to-artist discovery |
| Twitter/X | Standard account | Networking, artist outreach |
| Facebook | Page (not personal profile) | Secondary — house music communities exist in FB Groups |

### Email marketing (free tiers)

| Tool | Free tier limits | Use for |
|------|----------------|---------|
| Mailchimp | 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month | Email list for beat announcements, flash sales |
| Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) | Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day | Higher send volume than Mailchimp on free tier |

Pick one and stay with it. Mailchimp has a better visual editor; Brevo has a higher send limit. Both are more than enough to start.

### Analytics

| Google Analytics | Free, full-featured | Add to Carrd site or any custom website to track traffic |

### Content creation

| Tool | Free tier | Use for |

| Wavve.co | Limited videos per month | Audio waveform visualizer videos for YouTube and Instagram |

### Email capture gates (free beat for email)

| Tool | Free tier | Use for |
|------|----------|---------|
| Hypeddit | Free plan available | Gate a free beat download behind email capture or social follow |

### Music distribution (free or near-free)

## Section 2: The Daily 1-Hour System

Marketing does not require hours. It requires consistency. This 60-minute system, done every day, compounds into significant reach over 90 days.

### The 1-hour breakdown

| Time block | Task | Goal |
|-----------|------|------|
| 0–15 min | Create or schedule 1 piece of content | Stay present on algorithms |
| 15–30 min | Engage (comments, DMs, replies) | Build relationships + algorithm signals |
| 30–45 min | Outreach (3 artist DMs, 2 playlist pitches, 1 collab DM) | Direct pipeline to potential buyers |
| 45–60 min | Platform maintenance (SC upload or metadata update, BeatStars tag review) | Improve existing asset discoverability |

### Content creation in 15 minutes
This only works with preparation. Keep a content queue: a folder of beat clips already cut to 30 seconds, already exported from CapCut. Creating content takes 2 hours on Sunday so that it only takes 15 minutes each day to schedule and post.

### Engagement rules
- Never comment "fire" or single-word reactions — they read as bot behavior
- Comment on 5 posts from target artists (people who would buy beats)
- Reply to every comment on your own posts within 24 hours
- Like and respond to Stories from the 20 artists you are currently building a relationship with

### Outreach numbers
3 DMs per day = 21 per week = 90 per month. Even a 5% response rate = 4–5 conversations with potential buyers every month. At 1% conversion to sale, that is at least one sale per month from DM outreach alone — with zero money spent.

## Section 3: The Weekly Content Factory

Four producers generating a week of content in one 2-hour Sunday session.

### Sunday content session structure

**Hour 1: Film everything**

| Time | Task | Who |
|------|------|-----|
| 0–15 min | Each producer cuts a 30-second clip of their best beat this week | All 4 |
| 15–30 min | Each producer records one 60-second "making a beat" screen capture | All 4 |
| 30–45 min | Group: one shared "studio session" TikTok/Reel (all 4 in frame for 15 seconds) | All 4 together |
| 45–60 min | Each producer creates 1 thumbnail and 1 cover art in Canva for their beat | All 4 |

**Hour 2: Edit and schedule everything**

| Time | Task | Who |
|------|------|-----|
| 0–20 min | Edit all 4 beat clips in CapCut — add text overlays, color grade, export | 1 person (rotating editor role) |
| 20–40 min | Schedule all posts in Buffer or Later — assign days/times for the week | 1 person (rotating scheduler) |
| 40–60 min | Each producer uploads 1 beat to BeatStars + SC, writes descriptions | All 4 individually |

Output from 2 hours:
- 4 beat clip Reels/TikToks (one per producer)
- 4 process videos (repurpose across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts)
- 1 group content piece
- 4 new beats uploaded to stores
- A full week of posts scheduled and ready

### Platform content split for the week

| Platform | Posts per week | Type |
|----------|--------------|------|
| Instagram Reels | 4–5 | Beat clips + process |
| Instagram Stories | Daily (3–5/day) | Behind scenes, polls, links |
| TikTok | 7–14 (1–2/day) | Beat clips, process, duets |
| YouTube | 2 long-form + daily Shorts | Beats + tutorials |
| SoundCloud | 3 tracks | Free beats + tagged previews |
| Twitter/X | Daily 1–3 tweets | Engagement + beat drops |

## Section 4: The Monthly Revenue Push

At the end of each month, execute this sequence to maximize the month's final sales.

### Week 4, Day 1–2: Flash sale
- Set 20% off all leases on BeatStars (48-hour window)
- Announce on Instagram Stories (countdown sticker)
- Post on TikTok: "48-hour sale starts now"
- Send email to list: "Subscribers get 2-hour early access"
- Post on Twitter/X with the sale details

### Week 4, Day 3: Scarcity post
- "3 exclusives left at this price — after this week the price goes up"
- This is not false urgency — exclusives are genuinely limited to one buyer

### Week 4, Day 4: "Beat of the month" email
- Send one email to your full list with your best beat of the month
- Include: BeatStars link, a 60-second preview clip embedded or linked, a one-paragraph description of the vibe
- Subject line: "Best beat we made this month — [Beat Name]"

### Week 4, Day 5–7: Recap content
- Post: "This month in numbers" — plays, sales, new followers (social proof)
- Screenshot notable moments (first sale, follower milestone, artist using your beat)
- This content serves double duty: celebrates wins and demonstrates momentum to potential buyers

## Section 5: Platform Priority by Stage

Do not try to dominate every platform at once. Focus compounds growth. Move to the next platform when the current one has traction.

| Stage | Followers (combined) | Primary platform | Secondary | Reason |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|-----------|--------|
| Foundation | 0–100 | SoundCloud | TikTok | SC = producer-to-artist community; TikTok = viral potential with zero audience |
| Early growth | 100–500 | TikTok | Instagram | TikTok algorithm heavily favors new, small accounts; free organic reach |
| Building community | 500–2K | Instagram | YouTube | Instagram builds loyal community; YouTube builds SEO asset |
| Scaling | 2K–10K | All four equally | Email list | Diversify reach; start building owned audience via email |
| Established | 10K+ | Email + YouTube | All social | Own the audience; email list and YouTube are algorithm-independent |

### Why this order matters

SoundCloud first: it is where artists go to find beats and where producers network. Your first beats get real feedback from the people most likely to buy.

TikTok second: no following required to get reach. The algorithm is democratic for new accounts. Use this window — TikTok favors new accounts more than established ones for a while.

Instagram third: the community you built via TikTok converts to Instagram followers who engage more deeply. Instagram is where clients check you out before making a purchase decision.

YouTube fourth: YouTube takes 6–12 months to build meaningful organic search traffic. Start early, but do not prioritize it over faster channels when you are at zero.

Email last: counter-intuitive, but you need something to offer (free beat, catalog, catalog link) before an email list is worth building. Start collecting when you have a real reason for people to stay subscribed.

## Section 7: 30-Day Sprint Plan

A zero-budget sprint from complete zero to first sale.

| Day | Tasks |
|-----|-------|
| 1 | Set up BeatStars (free), Airbit, SoundCloud, Linktree, Mailchimp. Upload 5 beats to each store. Complete all profile fields. |
| 2 | Set up Instagram (Creator), TikTok, YouTube channel. Complete all profiles. Same username everywhere. |
| 3 | Create Canva templates: beat cover art, Instagram Reel cover, YouTube thumbnail. |
| 4 | Upload first 3 SC tracks (free beats, gated with follow). Post first Instagram Reel (beat reveal). Post first TikTok (beat process video). |
| 5 | Engage: comment on 20 artists' posts on Instagram + SoundCloud. Follow 20 relevant accounts on each platform. |
| 6 | Post second Reel. Post TikTok. Upload to YouTube (your first beat video). Respond to all comments from days 4–5. |
| 7 | Sunday: Run the weekly content factory session. Batch content for the coming week. |
| 8 | Schedule the week's posts. Start the daily 1-hour system today. DM 3 artists on SoundCloud. |
| 9 | Post Reel + TikTok. Upload second YouTube beat video. Set up Hypeddit gate on a free beat. |
| 10 | Engage day: comment on 20 more artists. Reply to every comment on your posts. |
| 11 | Post beat clip Reel. Post TikTok process video. Send first DM outreach to 3 artists on Instagram. |
| 12 | Upload SC tracks (2 more). Update descriptions with BeatStars links. Post YouTube Short. |
| 13 | Post Reel. DM follow-up to any SC/Instagram outreach that went warm. |
| 14 | Sunday: Content factory session. Week 2 complete. Review: which post performed best? Double down. |
| 15 | Begin Duet strategy on TikTok. Find 3 acapella vocalists and duet their videos with your beat. |
| 16 | Post Reel + TikTok. Upload a YouTube tutorial (10 min, "making a beat from scratch"). |
| 17 | Engage: 20 comments on target artists. Pin your best comment on your top TikTok. |
| 18 | Post Reel. Start tracking weekly stats for the first time (see Section 8). |
| 19 | DM 3 artists on Twitter/X. Post engagement tweet in music producer community. |
| 20 | Post beat clip + TikTok. Upload 3rd YouTube beat video. Reply to all comments. |
| 21 | Sunday: Content factory. Set up first flash sale for next week (prep the discount codes). |
| 22 | Post flash sale announcement on all platforms. Stories countdown sticker. |
| 23 | First sale may happen here — if not, continue. Mid-sale engagement post. |
| 24 | Last-chance flash sale reminder (24h left). Post on all platforms. |
| 25 | Flash sale ends. Post a "thank you" story. Announce next beat drop date. |
| 26 | Post Reel + TikTok. Upload beat making tutorial #2 to YouTube. |
| 27 | DM outreach: follow up with anyone who engaged with your flash sale posts but didn't buy. Offer a 1-time personal discount code. |
| 28 | Sunday: Content factory. Plan month-end recap. |
| 29 | Post month recap content: stats, wins, what's coming. |
| 30 | Send first email to email list (everyone who signed up via Hypeddit gate). Include your best beat of the month and a BeatStars link. |

If no sale by day 30: the system is working — it takes time. Identify the weakest link (profile views? Click-throughs? Beat page conversions?) and fix that specifically. Do not restart — iterate.

### What the numbers tell you

| If this metric is flat/low | Then fix this |
|---------------------------|--------------|
| BeatStars profile views | Your external marketing isn't driving traffic — audit your Linktree CTR |
| BeatStars plays | Beats are not getting listened to — fix titles, tags, thumbnails |
