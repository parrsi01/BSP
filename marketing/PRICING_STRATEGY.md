# Beat Pricing Playbook

---

## Market Rate Research Methodology

Before setting prices, know what the market looks like. Do this research quarterly.

### Research Steps
1. Go to [beatstars.com](https://www.beatstars.com) → search "deep house" (or your subgenre)
2. Filter by **New Releases** and **Top Selling** — check both
3. Open the top 20 producer profiles in your genre
4. Create a spreadsheet: Producer | Basic MP3 | Premium WAV | Unlimited | Exclusive

### What You'll Find (Typical 2024 Ranges)

| License | Low End | Mid Market | Premium |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Basic MP3 | $15 | $29.99 | $49.99 |
| Premium WAV | $29.99 | $49.99 | $79.99 |
| Unlimited | $59.99 | $99.99 | $149.99 |
| Exclusive | $99.99 | $299.99 | $500–$2,000+ |

**Insight:** House music is slightly less saturated than trap/hip-hop on BeatStars. You can price at mid-market without fighting as much competition.

## Price Anchoring: 3-Tier Display

Always show 3 license tiers side-by-side on your product page. This is a psychological pricing technique:

```
┌──────────────┬──────────────┬──────────────┐
│  Basic       │  Premium     │  Unlimited   │
│  MP3 Lease   │  WAV Lease   │  License     │
│              │              │              │
│  $29.99      │  $49.99      │  $99.99      │
│              │ ← Most Popular               │
│  • MP3 file  │  • WAV file  │  • WAV + MP3 │
│  • 2,500 str │  • 10K str   │  • Unlimited │
│  • 500 DL    │  • 1,000 DL  │  • Unlimited │
│  • 1 video   │  • 1 video   │  • Unlimited │
└──────────────┴──────────────┴──────────────┘
```

### Why This Works
- The **lowest tier** makes the middle tier look reasonable
- The **highest tier** makes the middle tier look like a deal
- The **"Most Popular" badge** on the middle tier pushes buyers toward it (social proof)
- Buyers rarely choose the cheapest option when a middle option exists with a clear value jump

## Bundle Deals

### Beat Bundle Structures That Sell

| Bundle | Price | Savings vs Individual |
|--------|-------|----------------------|
| 3 MP3 leases | $59.99 (vs $90) | 33% |
| 5 MP3 leases | $99.99 (vs $150) | 33% |
| 3 WAV leases | $99.99 (vs $150) | 33% |
| "Starter Pack" (10 MP3) | $149.99 (vs $300) | 50% |
| Genre pack (5 deep house) | $129.99 | 35% |

### How to Create Bundles on BeatStars
- BeatStars has a "Collections" feature — group beats by genre or mood
- Alternatively, sell bundles directly via Gumroad (simpler for custom packages)
- List the bundle as a separate product with its own cover art

### Themed Bundles
- "Deep House Summer Pack" — 5 beats with a summer/warm vibe
- "Studio Session Vol. 1" — 10 beats across subgenres
- "Afro House Essentials" — niche genre focus

## Flash Sale Strategy

### When to Run Flash Sales
- **New producer announcement:** 48-hour launch sale (25% off everything)
- **Holiday sales:** Black Friday, New Year's, summer
- **Milestone sales:** "100 beats uploaded — 24hr celebration sale"
- **Re-engagement:** If BeatStars traffic dips, a flash sale creates urgency

### Flash Sale Execution
1. Set the discount in BeatStars pricing settings
2. Post on all social platforms with a **countdown timer** (Instagram Stories countdown sticker)
3. Send to your email list first (subscribers get 2 hours early access = reward)
4. After 2 hours, post publicly
5. Post a "Last chance — 6 hours left" reminder

### Flash Sale Rules
- **Never run continuous sales** — it devalues your catalog. Max 1 per month.
- **Always anchor to the original price:** "Usually $49.99 — today only $29.99"
- **Time limit:** 24–48 hours maximum. Urgency drives action.

## Free Beat → Upsell Strategy

This is the highest-converting funnel for beat sellers.

### The Funnel
```
Free beat (tagged MP3) offered on social
  → Artist downloads and uses the beat
  → They upload to streaming or YouTube
  → Beat gets attention / grows
  → You DM or email: "Hey, I see you used my beat — want the untagged version + WAV stems?"
  → Upsell to non-exclusive WAV or exclusive
```

### Proactive Upsell Outreach
After releasing a free beat:
1. Monitor SoundCloud/YouTube for uploads using your beat (Google Alerts: "beat name" + your tag)
2. When an artist posts something getting traction, reach out
3. DM: "Saw your track doing well — want to upgrade to WAV/exclusive before someone else buys it?"

### The Urgency Script
For non-exclusive beats: "I've had 3 people purchase a lease on this — if you want exclusive rights before anyone else locks it down, the price is X"

## Exclusive vs Non-Exclusive Economics

### Scenario Analysis

Assume a beat sells 20 non-exclusive leases over 12 months:

| License | Price | Total Revenue (20 sales) |
|---------|-------|------------------------|
| Basic MP3 | $29.99 | $599.80 |
| Premium WAV | $49.99 | $999.80 |
| Mixed (10/10) | — | $799.80 |

Compare to:
| Exclusive Sale | Price | Revenue |
|---------------|-------|---------|
| Immediate exclusive | $299.99 | $299.99 |

**Insight:** If you believe a beat can sell 10+ non-exclusive leases, declining an exclusive below $500 is often financially rational. But if the artist has a significant following and could make the beat famous, the exposure value of an exclusive may exceed the dollars.

### When to Accept a Low Exclusive Offer
- The artist has 50K+ genuine followers and real traction
- The placement could lead to more sales of your other beats
- The beat has been sitting unsold for 6+ months
- The artist is a known collaborator who could send more clients your way

## When to Raise Prices

### Signs You Should Raise Prices

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Consistently selling 3+ leases/week on a beat | Raise that beat's price by $10–$20 |
| 0 complaints about price in DMs | Market accepts the current price — test higher |
| Getting exclusive offers above list price | Raise exclusive price |
| New followers from a viral post | Strike while hot — raise temporarily |
| 6 months experience with good reviews | Time for a general price increase |

### How to Raise Prices Without Losing Buyers
1. Announce it in advance: "Prices going up on [date]"
2. Offer a **last chance at old prices** for 48 hours (this creates a buying event)
3. Raise prices during a period of high activity (after a viral post)
4. Don't apologize for it — higher prices signal higher quality
