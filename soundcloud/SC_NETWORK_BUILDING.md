# SoundCloud Network Building — Producers, Curators, Artists

---

## Section 1: Producer-to-Producer Networks

### How to Find Producers to Network With

**Search method:**
1. SC search → type your genre → filter by "People"
2. Sort by "Most followed" — these are established accounts worth connecting with
3. Also sort by "Recent activity" — these are active accounts that will actually respond
4. Target: producers with 500–10K followers in your exact genre (they're hungry and responsive)
5. Avoid: producers with 50K+ followers for initial outreach — the response rate is near zero

**Filter method:**
1. Find a track in your genre with 5K+ plays
2. Open the likes/reposts list
3. Every liker/reposter who is a producer (not a listener) is a potential network partner
4. These accounts have already demonstrated they engage with your niche

---

### Repost Exchange Setup

**DM template — cold repost exchange pitch:**
```
Hey [name], big fan of [specific track or style]. I'm also producing [genre] —
check out [your track link] if you get a chance.

I do weekly repost exchanges with a small group of producers in the same lane.
Interested? We rotate — one drop per person per week, everyone reposts within 2h.

Completely free, no commitment beyond trying it once.
```

**Rotation schedule (5-person group):**

| Week | Who Drops | Everyone Else Does |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | Producer A | Reposts A's track within 2 hours |
| Week 2 | Producer B | Reposts B's track within 2 hours |
| Week 3 | Producer C | Reposts C's track |
| Week 4 | Producer D | Reposts D's track |
| Week 5 | Producer E | Reposts E's track |

**Rules for the rotation:**
- One track per producer per rotation — no flooding
- Must repost within 2 hours of the drop (captures engagement velocity)
- No obligation to repost if the track quality is off-brand for you (protect your curation)
- Group decides by vote when to add new members

**Group size management:**
- 3–5 producers: easy to manage, WhatsApp group works fine
- 6–15 producers: Discord channel with a bot for scheduling
- 16–50 producers: Structured Discord server with roles, submission threads, and a schedule pinboard

---

### Beat Feedback Circles

A weekly feedback session keeps the network engaged and improves everyone's product.

**Structure:**
- 5–8 producers per circle
- Each person drops an unreleased WIP (work-in-progress) link
- Feedback format: 2 positives + 1 specific improvement suggestion
- WhatsApp voice notes or Discord voice channel (Thursday evenings work well)
- Duration: 45–60 minutes maximum

**Feedback template (what to cover):**
1. First 8 bars — does the hook work?
2. Mix clarity — is anything clashing?
3. Energy arc — does the beat build well?
4. Market fit — which artists could use this?

### Collab Production

**Finding collab partners:**
- Post in Discord producer servers: "Looking for collab partner, [genre], 120–128 BPM, DM stems"
- Reddit r/WeAreTheMusicMakers monthly collab threads (posted every month — search "[month] collab thread")
- Direct SC DM to producers whose sound complements yours (not identical)

**Split agreements — before you start:**

| Term | Standard Split | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Revenue | 50/50 | Equal contribution |
| Publishing | 50/50 | Always match revenue split |
| Masters | Shared equally | Both names on the upload |
| Exclusives | Must agree jointly | One person can't sell exclusive without other's consent |

**Simple collab agreement (text format, no lawyer needed for under $500 deals):**
```
COLLAB AGREEMENT — [Date]
Producers: [Name 1] and [Name 2]
Track: [Working title]
Revenue split: 50/50 on all licenses
Exclusive sales: require both parties to agree
Masters: co-owned
Publishing: co-owned
Valid for: all licenses until one party requests renegotiation in writing
```
Screenshot this and both producers confirm in writing (DM or email).

---

### Producer Crew Branding

**Naming your collective:**
- Keep it short (2–3 syllables)
- Avoid numbers unless they're meaningful
- Must be searchable — check SC, Instagram, Twitter for conflicts before committing
- Examples: BSP, AHK Collective, Dark Room Beats, Voltage Gang

**Shared aesthetic:**
- All crew tracks use same artwork template (Canva — free, build a template)
- Same font, same color palette across all producer pages
- Shared hashtag: #BSPBeats used consistently on all platforms by all crew members

**Crew page on SC:**
- Create a second SC account as the crew label page
- All crew members upload collab tracks here
- Crew page reposts individual member tracks = extra reach for everyone
- Label page builds its own following independently

### Building a WhatsApp/Discord Group for Your Repost Network

**WhatsApp (for 3–10 producers, no tech overhead):**
- Create group, name it clearly: "BSP Repost Network [Genre]"
- Pinned message = rotation schedule and rules
- Dedicated "drops" thread for track links only
- Separate "general" thread for conversation

**Discord (for 10+ producers, better organization):**
```
Server structure:
#announcements — admin posts rotation schedule
#drop-submissions — post your track here on your drop week
#feedback — optional WIP feedback
#general — off-topic conversation
#resources — useful tools, articles, tutorials
Voice channel: "Weekly Listening Session"
```

**Bot tools:** MEE6 (free) handles scheduled announcements and moderation.

---

### Scaling from 5 to 50 Network Members

| Stage | Size | Management | Recruitment |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Bootstrap | 3–5 | Group chat | Hand-picked |
| Growth | 6–15 | Discord | Members refer 1–2 others |
| Established | 16–30 | Discord + mod | Application form (Typeform free) |
| Large | 31–50 | Discord + multiple mods | Curated, genre-specific |

**Application screening (for stage 3+):**
- Require: SC link, 3 sample tracks, follower count
- Minimum bar: 200+ followers, active in last 30 days, consistent genre fit
- Reject: accounts with bot followers, accounts in mismatched genres, inactive accounts

## Section 2: Curator Relationships

### Identifying Real Curators vs Inactive Playlist Owners

| Real Curator | Inactive Owner |
|-------------|----------------|
| Playlist updated in last 3 months | Last update 6+ months ago |
| Active SC account (posts, reposts) | Profile has no activity |
| Consistent theme across playlist | Random mix of unrelated genres |
| Responds to DMs (check their bio for contact) | No bio, no contact info |
| Playlist has 50+ tracks (invested effort) | Playlist under 10 tracks |

**Finding active curators:**
1. SC search: "[genre] playlist 2025" — filter by Playlists
2. Open playlist → check "Last updated" date
3. Click curator's name → view their recent activity
4. Check follower count of playlist (not curator) — 200+ playlist followers = real traction

---

### Cold vs Warm Outreach

**Warm outreach (3x higher response rate):**
1. Follow the curator's main account
2. Leave a genuine comment on one of their recently posted tracks (not the playlist — their personal posts)
3. Like 2–3 of their tracks
4. Wait 3–5 days
5. Then DM your pitch — you're no longer a stranger

**Pitch DM template:**
```
Hey [name], I've been enjoying [playlist name] —
especially the recent [artist/track] addition.

I produce [genre] and just finished something I think fits
the energy of your playlist. Link: [track link]

No pressure — even if it's not the right fit,
always good to connect with other [genre] people on here.
```

---

### Curator Contact List — Notion Template Structure

Build a running database of every curator you've contacted or plan to contact:

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Curator name | SC display name |
| SC profile link | Direct URL |
| Playlist name | Exact name |
| Playlist followers | Number |
| Last playlist update | Date |
| Genre fit (1–5) | How well your music fits |
| Outreach status | Not contacted / Followed / Commented / Pitched / Added / Rejected |
| Pitch date | When you sent the DM |
| Response | Yes/No/Pending |
| Notes | Any personalization details |

Update this weekly. Aim to add 5 new curators per week to your pipeline.

---

### Following Up Without Being Annoying

**Follow-up timeline:**
- Day 0: Send pitch DM
- Day 7: If no response — one short follow-up: "Hey [name] — just wanted to bump this in case it got lost. Happy to send a different track if [linked one] isn't quite right."
- Day 14: No further follow-up. Move to "cold" in your tracker.

**Rule:** Two messages maximum, ever. Curators remember spammers and will actively ignore future outreach.

---

### Growing Your Own Curated Playlist to 1K+ Followers

Running your own genre playlist is a long-term asset that compounds.

**Steps:**
1. Create playlist with a keyword-rich name: "Afro House Essentials 2025"
2. Seed it with 15–20 tracks (70% established artists, 30% up-and-comers)
3. Post it on Reddit r/housemusic with context: "I curated this afro house playlist..."
4. Reach out to the producers in your playlist: "I added your track to [playlist name] — [link]"
5. Many of those producers will share the playlist = free follower growth
6. Update monthly with 3–5 new tracks and repost the playlist update

**Growth target:** 20–30 new followers/month on the playlist. At 1K followers, the playlist becomes an asset you can use as a reciprocal offer to other curators.

---

### Curator Reciprocity

The most sustainable curator relationship is mutual:
- You feature their track/recommendation in your playlist
- They feature your track in their playlist

**How to propose reciprocity:**
```
Hey [name], I added [their track] to my [your playlist name] (it's got [X] followers).
Would love to be featured in [their playlist] sometime when I have something that fits.
No rush — just thought I'd mention it.
```

This works because it leads with value delivered, not value requested.

## Section 3: Artist Relationships

### Scouting Artists on SC Who Need Beats

**Search method:**
1. SC search → your genre → filter "Tracks" → sort "Recent"
2. Look for tracks that are clearly original music (vocals, lyrics) not instrumentals
3. Check the uploader's profile — are they an artist (performs, writes) or a producer?
4. Artist signals: they have a photo, original vocal tracks, lyrics in description, references to shows or features

**Qualification checklist for a target artist:**
- [ ] Original vocal music (not just covers)
- [ ] Active in last 30 days
- [ ] 50–10K followers
- [ ] Music style matches your production
- [ ] Does not already have a dedicated producer they're locked into

---

### The 50–10K Follower Sweet Spot

| Follower Range | Why Target Them |
|---------------|-----------------|
| 0–50 | Too early — no budget, no audience, low conversion |
| 50–500 | Emerging — will accept free beats, low purchase intent |
| 500–5K | Sweet spot — enough audience to need quality, no label budget yet |
| 5K–10K | Ideal buyer — proven audience, budget for leases, high intent |
| 10K–50K | Can buy — approaching label deals, more selective |
| 50K+ | Likely managed — go through management, not SC DM |

---

### Building Long-Term Artist Relationships

One-time beat sales have low ROI. Long-term artist relationships compound.

**Relationship-building sequence:**
1. Comment genuinely on 3 of their tracks before any sales pitch
2. First contact: share a beat for free reference ("no strings, just thought this fits your sound")
3. If they use it and tag you, reciprocate with another free beat or a discounted lease
4. After 2–3 interactions, propose a formal arrangement: you produce for them regularly

**Long-term value signals to send:**
- Remember their song names in conversations
- Congratulate them on milestones (new single drop, follower milestone)
- Send them beats before releasing to the public ("first look" for trusted artists)

---

### The "Producer on Call" Model

A producer on call is an artist's exclusive or preferred producer — they come to you first for every project.

**How to become someone's go-to producer:**
1. Learn their key signature preferences, BPM range, reference artists
2. Build a private folder of beats specifically for them (use Dropbox or Google Drive)
3. Deliver fast — 24-hour turnaround on beat requests builds irreplaceable trust
4. Price as a package: "10 beats/month for $200" beats selling individual leases

**Package pricing examples:**
- Monthly beat subscription: $150–$300/month for 4–8 custom beats
- Album deal: $500–$1,500 for full album production (10–14 tracks)
- Retainer: $200/month, you stay available for revisions + new ideas

---

### How to Approach Signed Artists

**Major label artists:** Do not cold DM. Their management filters everything. The path is: get big enough that A&Rs find you, or pitch through sync licensing channels.

**Indie label artists (independent, distributed via DistroKid/TuneCore):**
- These artists run their own careers and check their own DMs
- Same warm outreach approach as unsigned artists
- Slightly higher budget available — price exclusives at $500+
- Reference their distributor or label in your pitch to show you've done research

---

### When to Offer Free Beats (Who Is Worth It)

| Offer Free If | Don't Offer Free If |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Artist has 5K+ engaged followers | Artist has zero releases |
| Artist has editorial playlist placements | Artist hasn't responded to 2 previous DMs |
| Artist is touring or has press coverage | Artist is asking everyone for free beats |
| You want a specific co-sign or placement | They can clearly afford a lease |
| They've already purchased from you before | The genre doesn't fit your brand |

**Free beat ROI calculation:** A free beat to an artist with 10K followers who posts it = potentially 500–2K new ears on your production credit = 5–10 profile visits = 1–3 new followers/buyers.

---

### Artist DM Scripts — 5 Scenarios

**1. Cold outreach (never commented before):**
```
Hey [name], just found your page — [specific track] caught me off guard in the best way.

I make [genre] beats and I think I have something that fits your sound.
Link here if you're interested: [track link]

No strings — just wanted to share it.
```

**2. Warm outreach (after commenting):**
```
Hey [name], left a comment on [track name] earlier — love the direction you're taking.
I've been making [genre] beats and just finished something I think fits where you're headed.
[Track link] — let me know what you think.
```

**3. Collab pitch:**
```
Hey [name], been following your work for a while. I'm a [genre] producer
and I think a collab between us would be something different.

I'd produce a custom track for you — no fee, just a co-credit and split if it gets licensed.
Open to it?
```

**4. Exclusive pitch:**
```
Hey [name], I have a beat I've been holding — it's the kind of thing
I only want on one project. Feels made for your sound.

I could hold it for 48 hours before leasing it publicly.
Interested in hearing it first? [Link]
```

**5. Follow-up (no response after first message):**
```
Hey [name] — just wanted to bump this in case it got buried.
Not trying to push anything — if the timing or sound isn't right, totally understand.
```

## Section 4: Label & Sync Contacts

### Finding Indie Labels on SC

**Search method:**
- SC search: "[genre] records", "[genre] label", "[city] music label"
- Look for accounts with: "Label" or "Records" in name, multiple artists on roster, consistent release schedule
- Signs of a real label: they repost from multiple different artists, have a PR email in bio, professional artwork

**How to pitch a label page:**
- Do not DM cold immediately
- Follow + repost 2–3 of their roster's tracks first
- Then pitch: "I produce [genre] — I've noticed you release [type of music]. Here's my catalog: [link]. Open to discussing placements."

---

### Sync Licensing Contacts Who Browse SC

Music supervisors (for film, TV, ads) do browse SoundCloud, particularly for electronic/underground genres that feel too independent for traditional pitching.

**Making your SC profile sync-discoverable:**
1. Tag tracks with: `instrumental`, `no samples`, `sync ready`, `TV music`, `film score`
2. Include in description: "Cleared for sync licensing. No interpolations. Original composition."
3. Make a dedicated "Sync Ready" playlist with your cleanest, most cinematic/commercial instrumentals
4. Add your sync contact email prominently in bio

**The "sync clean" requirement:**
- No uncleared samples — this eliminates most beats immediately
- Original melody, original drums, original chords
- This is why original production (not sample-based) has higher sync value

---

### How to Tag Tracks for Sync Discovery

| Sync Context | Tags to Add |
|-------------|-------------|
| Corporate/ad music | `corporate`, `background music`, `business`, `motivational` |
| Film/drama | `cinematic`, `dramatic`, `film score`, `emotional` |
| TV montage | `montage`, `uplifting`, `inspiring` |
| Sports content | `hype`, `energy`, `workout`, `sports` |
| Fashion/luxury | `elegant`, `sophisticated`, `luxury`, `fashion` |

Keep these as secondary tags on tracks that genuinely fit. Don't tag every beat as "cinematic."

---

### Music Supervisor Outreach from SC Profile

The realistic sync path from SoundCloud is indirect:
1. Build a catalog of sync-ready tracks on SC (well-tagged, professional)
2. Create a dedicated sync licensing page on your Carrd site with embedded SC player
3. Pitch to sync libraries (Musicbed, Artlist, Pond5) — they have SC submission portals or email intake
4. Pitch directly to music supervisors via LinkedIn (not SC DM — supervisors use LinkedIn)
5. Use your SC as the demo portfolio that a supervisor can preview when they receive your LinkedIn pitch
