# SoundCloud Collaboration — Full Playbook

---

## 1. Types of Collabs on SoundCloud

Understanding which collab type fits your goal before reaching out prevents wasted outreach and mismatched expectations.

| Collab Type | What It Is | Best For |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Beat swap | You make a beat for them, they make one for you | Network building, no revenue share complexity |
| Co-production | Both producers work on one track together | Genuine creative synergy, shared audiences |
| Feature collab | Vocalist performs on your instrumental | Beat activation, content that gets more plays than instrumentals |
| Remix exchange | You remix their track, they remix yours | Double the content, double the promotion events |
| Joint playlist | Both curate one playlist, both promote it | Shared curation audience, low effort/high reach |
| Label/imprint release | Release under a shared imprint name | Brand building, catalog depth, looks like a label |

## 2. Finding Collab Partners

### SoundCloud Search Method
1. SC search → your genre → filter "People" → sort "Most active"
2. Look for producers with similar follower counts (within 50% of yours)
3. Quality check: listen to their top 3 tracks — do you genuinely like the sound?
4. Engage first: comment on one of their tracks before sending any pitch

**Complementary vs identical sound:**
- Collab with producers who complement you, not who sound exactly like you
- Example: You make afro house with heavy drums → collab with someone who brings strong melody/chords
- The combination should produce something neither of you would make alone

## 3. Collab DM Templates

**Template 1 — Beat Swap (SC DM):**
```
Hey [name], been listening to your stuff — [specific track] hits different.

I'm a [genre] producer and I think our sounds would complement each other.
Down for a beat swap? I send you a beat, you send me one — no money,
just good music and cross-promotion.

Here's a sample of what I make: [SC link]
```

**Template 2 — Co-Production:**
```
Hey [name], your [specific element — chords/drums/vibe] on [track name] was exactly
what I've been trying to build toward.

I have an idea that I think would work better as a two-producer thing.
I'd bring [your strengths] and I think you'd add [what you see in their music].

Open to a test collab — no commitment, just see what comes out?
```

**Template 3 — Feature (for vocalists):**
```
Hey [name], your voice on [track name] is exactly the energy I've been looking for.

I produce [genre] and I have a beat that I wrote with this kind of vocal in mind.
Would you be open to jumping on it? I handle all the production and mixing;
you'd get full vocal credit and 50% of any revenue.

Beat is here: [SC link]
```

**Template 4 — Remix Exchange:**
```
Hey [name], I'd love to do a remix exchange with you.
You remix one of my tracks, I remix one of yours — both upload on the same day,
tag each other, promote to our audiences.

Double the content, double the reach. Interested?
```

**Template 5 — Joint Playlist:**
```
Hey [name], you curate some of the best [genre] I've found on SC.

What do you think about building a joint playlist? We each add tracks we love,
call it something like "[Genre] by [Your name] + [Their name]" — both promote it.
Low effort, and your followers get introduced to my picks and vice versa.
```

## 4. Split Agreements

Settle these five questions before starting any collab. Do it in writing — a DM thread or email is sufficient for small deals.

| Term | Standard Split | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Revenue split | 50/50 equal contribution | Adjust if one person contributes significantly more |
| Revenue split (minor contribution) | 70/30 (major/minor) | Clear upfront when one person adds a small element |
| Publishing | Matches revenue split | Always |
| Masters ownership | Co-owned | Both names on the upload |
| Exclusive sales | Requires both parties' consent | One person cannot sell exclusive without agreement from both |
| Derivative works (remixes, samples) | Both must approve | Prevents either party from licensing the collab to a third party without discussion |

### Simple Collab Agreement (Text Format — No Lawyer Required for Under $500 Deals)

```
COLLAB AGREEMENT
Date: [Date]
Producers: [Name 1] ("Producer A") and [Name 2] ("Producer B")
Track working title: [Title]

1. REVENUE: All licensing income split [50/50 or agreed ratio]
2. PUBLISHING: Same split as revenue
3. MASTERS: Co-owned equally by both producers
4. EXCLUSIVES: Neither party may sell or license as exclusive without written consent from both
5. DERIVATIVES: Neither party may authorize remixes or interpolations without agreement
6. CREDITS: Both credited as: "Produced by [Name 1] x [Name 2]"
7. TERM: This agreement applies to all uses of the track listed above, indefinitely

Agreed by: [Both confirm "agreed" in DM or reply to email]
```

Screenshot the confirmation thread. This protects both parties from disputes later.

## 5. Cross-Promotion Structure

A collab without coordinated promotion leaves 50% of the value on the table.

**Launch day coordination:**

| Time | Both Producers Do |
|------|-----------------|
| T-3 days | Both post teaser to Instagram Stories: "Collab dropping [date]" |
| T-1 day | Both announce on Twitter/X: "Tomorrow: [genre] collab with [each other's @]" |
| Launch day 12:00 PM | Both upload to SC simultaneously |
| Launch day 12:05 PM | Both repost each other's upload |
| Launch day 12:10 PM | Both post to all socials (Instagram, TikTok, Twitter, Facebook) |
| Launch day: within 1 hour | Both pitch to their respective curator lists (the collab format is a strong pitch angle) |
| Day 3 | Both post a second social piece: waveform video or behind-the-scenes |
| Day 7 | Both share the play count ("reached X plays in one week") |

**Why same-day, simultaneous upload matters:** Both audiences see the collab at the same time. If Producer A uploads 3 days before Producer B, the momentum and velocity spike is split across two different events instead of compounding on one.

## 6. The Remix Pack Strategy

A remix pack inverts the standard collab model: you give other producers your stems, they produce something new, and every remixer becomes a promoter of your original.

**Implementation:**

**Step 1: Prepare the pack**
- Export your beat's stems (drums, bass, chords, melody, FX — individually)
- Package as a ZIP file with clear labeling
- Include a one-page PDF: "Remix Terms — tag BSP in your upload, credit original in description, no exclusive sales without our agreement"

**Step 2: Upload and announce**
- Upload the original beat as normal
- Add to description: "Remix pack available — DM for stems"
- Or: upload the stems pack as a free download with gate (Hypeddit — requires follow + repost)

**Step 3: Distribute to targeted producers**
- DM 10–15 producers in complementary genres: "I'm releasing a remix pack for [beat name] — interested in having your remix featured on my profile?"
- Send directly to producers whose remix style would be interesting

**Step 4: Feature the best remixes**
- Best 3–5 remixes get reposted to your main profile
- Each remixer promotes the remix to their audience → all roads lead back to the original
- Create a "Remix Pack Vol.1" playlist with original + all featured remixes

**Expected outcome:** 5 remixes by producers with 500–2K followers each = 2,500–10,000 additional ears on your original production.

## 7. Label / Imprint Setup on SoundCloud

### Creating a Label SoundCloud Page

1. Create a second SC account with your imprint name (e.g., "BSP Records", "BSP Collective")
2. Set up the profile identically to your main account in terms of quality (artwork, bio, links)
3. Bio format: "BSP Records — House & Trap Dancehall. Releases from [Producer names]. Demos: [email]"

**What to upload to the label page:**
- Collab tracks (upload once here, both individual producers repost it)
- Beat compilations and volumes
- Any release where multiple producers contributed

**What NOT to upload to the label page:**
- Individual solo beats that belong on your personal page
- Unfinished or experimental work

### Using a Label Page for Multi-Producer Teams

If BSP has multiple producers (or will in the future):

| Action | Individual Profile | Label Profile |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Solo beats | Upload here | Do not upload |
| Collabs / compilations | Co-upload or repost | Primary upload |
| Beat tapes | Upload here | Repost |
| Artist collaborations | Upload here | Repost if label-level quality |

**Label page = extra repost source:** Every track from any BSP producer gets reposted to BSP Records. The label's follower base grows over time and provides an additional repost reach for every team member's music.

## 8. Guest Curation

Invite a producer with more followers than you to "guest curate" one of your playlists for a week.

**Execution:**
1. Find a producer in an adjacent genre with 2–5x your followers
2. DM: "I run a curated playlist — [playlist link, X followers]. Would you be open to guest curating it for a week? Pick 5–10 tracks you love, I'll add them with your name as the guest curator. Both share the playlist."
3. If they agree: add their tracks, add "Curated this week by [Producer name]" to the playlist description
4. Both share the playlist on upload day
5. Their followers see the playlist — many discover you as the playlist owner

**Why bigger producers agree:**
- Free exposure for their own taste/curation
- They share content with their audience that isn't just self-promotion
- Zero work for them (you manage the logistics)

**Expected outcome:** A guest curator with 5K followers typically brings 200–500 playlist plays and 10–30 new followers to your profile. At scale, run this monthly with a different guest.

## 9. Collab Cadence and Tracking

Track all collab conversations and outcomes in a simple system:

### Collab Pipeline Tracker (Notion or Airtable)

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Name | Producer/artist name |
| SC link | Their profile |
| Genre | Their genre |
| Followers | Their follower count |
| Collab type proposed | Beat swap / co-prod / feature / remix |
| Status | Pitched / In progress / Live / Completed / Declined |
| Track link | SC link when live |
| Notes | Any important context |
| Last contact date | Prevent follow-up too early or too late |

**Cadence targets:**
- 5 new collab pitches per month
- 1–2 collabs live per month
- Review pipeline every 2 weeks

## 10. Collab Checklist — Before You Start

Before any work begins:

- [ ] Both producers have listened to and genuinely like each other's work
- [ ] Collab type agreed (co-prod, beat swap, feature, etc.)
- [ ] Revenue split agreed in writing
- [ ] Masters and publishing split agreed
- [ ] Launch date set tentatively
- [ ] Both agree on how exclusive sales are handled
- [ ] Credit format agreed: "Produced by [Name A] x [Name B]"
- [ ] Social handles exchanged for tagging

**After the track is complete:**
- [ ] Both upload (same day if co-production)
- [ ] Both repost each other within 2 hours
- [ ] Both post to socials within same hour
- [ ] Both pitch curators from their own lists
- [ ] Both link to each other's profiles in description
- [ ] Set a 14-day check-in to review plays and decide on any additional promotion
